## **12.2 Rules for Poison, Illness and Infection (part 2)**

In _Sword World RPG_, illness is defined as a state in which the balance of spirit power within the body is disrupted. The concept of bacterial or viral contagion does not exist. There are two types of symptoms that can appear. One is due to **poison** and the other is due to **illness**. In addition, a wound may become **infected**, which is treated as an illness.

Poisons force an imbalance in the spirit power within the body. As long as the poison remains in the body, even if the symptoms disappear, the balance of spirit power will be disrupted and the symptoms will appear again. However, if the poison itself is cleared away, the spirit power within the body will naturally return to a balanced state and restore health.

On the other hand, illness is a state where the spirit power within the body remains unbalanced and in a fixed state. Think of it as the spirits inside your body that are under the illusion that a false equilibrium is the correct one. Illness infection occurs when spirits transmit information about the incorrect equilibrium state to spirits in other human bodies. The unknown spirit of life heals illness by showing other spirits in the body how to achieve balance.

In _Sword World RPG_, the phenomenon of a wound becoming infected means that _the container was broken and deformed, causing the contents to become unbalanced_. The logic goes that the greater the wound, the greater the risk of infection.

## **12.2.1 Poisons**

This section explains the rules regarding poison.

## **Poison Treatment 12.2.1.1 and Detox Methods**

Just like in our world, in Alecrast, using poison as a weapon is an immoral act. If a player character uses poison to defeat an enemy, they are sure to be treated as a coward and looked down upon by the public. Therefore, a proud warrior does _not_ want to use poison. On the other hand, assassins, evil rune masters, dark priests, etc. _do_ like to use poison.

Poisons that have entered the body can be cleared using specific antidotes (if any), the holy magic _Cure Poison_ or _Refresh_, or the spirit magic _Restore Health_.

To remove a poison with magic, the caster's final score (magic power + 2D roll) must be greater than or equal to the poison's **toxicity score**. Once a detox fails, the same caster cannot attempt detox magic until their magic power increases (and even if they try, they will always fail).

Even if you detoxify someone _after_ they die from poison, they will _not_ come back to life. Some poisons have symptoms similar to illnesses, but they are _not_ natural illnesses and _cannot_ be cured with _Cure Disease_.

The spirit magic _Purification_ can make liquid poison harmless if the final score when casting the magic is greater than or equal to than the toxicity score. However, it's _not_ possible to drive off a poison that's _already_ entered the body.

## **12.2.1.2 Resistance to Poison**

Poisons are not effective against everything. It has no effect on monsters that have no life such as undead, golems, etc. Some mythical and magical beasts have strong life force that makes them immune to poison. Some poison effects may or may not affect specific monsters.

Monsters that use poison as weapons are immune to their _own_ poisons (but are still affected by _other_ poisons).

## **Usage**

The **usage** of the poison is written in parentheses after the name. These are divided into **injected**, **inhaled**, **dose**, and **touch**. _Injected_ poisons are poisons that need to be delivered directly into the bloodstream, usually in liquid form. It's applied to small daggers and needles and used as traps on doors and treasure chests. It can be applied to knives and arrows to increase their killing power, but the poison usually wears off after about three uses and loses its effect, so it must be reapplied frequently. Many creatures, such as giant scorpions, poisonous snakes, etc. use injected poison as a weapon. This type of poison only takes effect if you lose 1 or more points of life force when you are hurt by a poisoned blade. Even if the blade hits, if it doesn't cause a wound, it has no effect.

_Inhaled_ poisons are in gas or powder form and take effect when inhaled through the respiratory tract. This is also often used as a trap, but another method is to store it in a breakable bottle and use it by slamming it into your opponent. In rare cases, some creatures use poison gas as a weapon.

If you're trapped in a room filled with poison gas, you must make a resistance roll every round. If you hold your breath, you will not be affected by the gas, but of course you will not be able to speak or cast spells while holding your breath. An adventurer can only hold their breath for a number of rounds equal to their life force.

## **12.2.2.1 Details**

Illness details are expressed as follows.

## **[ Common Cold ]** 

Symptoms=Fever, cough, sneezing, runny nose, diarrhea, arthritis Rarity=5 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=1 day/5/7 (14) Fatal Depth=5 

Infection Power=Moderate 

This is what they call a cold. It's accompanied by a slight fever, cough, sneezing, runny nose, etc. Some people may experience diarrhea symptoms or joint pain. You suffer a -1 penalty on all success rolls while you have a cold. As the cold progresses, you may develop a high fever and also have difficulty breathing. 

## **Symptoms** 

This briefly describes the conditions that appear in the illness. 

## **Rarity** 

This score is used when making a check to see if you know the illness. If an owner of the sage skill or healer skill succeeds on a success roll using _skill level + intelligence bonus_ as the baseline score and **rarity** as the target score, they can look at a sick patient and identify which illness they have. This means not only 

knowing the name of the disease, but also its characteristics and how to deal with it. If a disease can be identified, owners of the healer skill can provide appropriate treatment. 

## **Progression Speed/Progression** 

## **Intensity/ Healing Score** 

After a certain period of time has elapsed after becoming ill, the illness either progresses further or gets better. The degree of progression of an illness is indicated by a score called **depth** . Immediately after contracting an illness, the _depth_ is 1. After the time indicated by **progression speed** has elapsed since you contracted the illness, roll two dice and look at the result. If the roll is less than the **progression intensity** , the disease gets worse and the _depth_ increases by 1. If the roll is greater than or equal to the **progression intensity** but less than the **healing score** , the illness neither worsens nor improves, and the _depth_ remains the same. If the result is greater than or equal to the **healing score_ , the illness will be cured and the _depth_ will be reduced by 1. When the _depth_ becomes 0, you are completely recovered. The patient's adventurer level and life force bonus have _no_ influence on this check. Adventurer level may also be hero level, but _even if you're a hero, you cannot win against illness_ . 

Even if a shaman cannot use the spell _Restore Health_ , they can still work with one type of spirit to alleviate symptoms. For example, by working with salamander, fever, which is the main symptom of a cold, can be alleviated. When a shaman performs such a treatment, they gain a +1 bonus on progression/healing checks. 

In addition to these, depending on the illness, there are herbs and special medicines that can provide bonuses to progression/healing checks. 

When the holy magic _Cure Disease_ or _Refresh_ , or the spirit magic _Restore Health_ are cast, the caster must make a success roll using their magic power as 

the baseline score. The target score is double the _healing score_ (added in parentheses). If you succeed, the _depth_ of the illness is reduced by 1 and you can make an additional success roll. You can continue to make this check until you fail or until the illness is completely cured. If this does _not_ completely cure the illness, it’ll be _useless_ to cast these spells on the same patient until the caster's magic power increases. 

If a healer who knows the true nature of an illness cares for the patient for more than one hour per day, they can make a similar check using that healer's _healer skill level + intelligence bonus_ as the baseline score. However, this must be at each progression speed of the illness (i.e. immediately after the illness's progression check), and you can only make one success roll. However, even if they fail, the same healer can continue to care for the patient. If after some time has passed, an illness progression check occurs, the healer can also make a healing check. 

## **Fatal Depth** 

If, as an illness progresses, its _depth_ reaches the **fatal depth** , the character will die from the illness. 

## **Infection Power** 

Indicates the infectiousness of the illness. If it's **very strong** , it has the infection power of a plague. **Strong** is about the same level as influenza. As in the example, **moderate** means it's just a cold, and **weak** means it's not very infectious. When it's **very weak** , it's not infectious and is unlikely to spread to other people. When an illness is _very strong_ or _strong_ , there is a high possibility that the infection will spread to the healer, etc. who is caring for the patient. If the game master determines that there is a possibility of contracting the illness, have the character make a progression check for the illness (compare a 2D roll with progression intensity). ## **12.2.2.1 Illness Samples** 

Here are some examples of illnesses seen in _Sword World_ . Most of the illnesses listed here are incurable diseases that are difficult to heal and have a high mortality rate. The game master may also create any other illnesses they desire. 

**[ Green Slime Rot ]** 

Symptoms=Green spots appear on the body, which eventually rots away from there Rarity=15 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=3 days/6/9 (18) Fatal Depth=3 

Infection Power=Moderate 

This is said to be an illness that occurs when the power of gnome in the body weakens and the power of undine becomes stronger. Immediately after infection, itchy green spots about 3 cm in diameter appear all over the body, but at _depth_ 2, the spots emit a putrid odor and a dirty pus begins to flow out. In this state, it's almost impossible to take action. When the lethal depth of 3 is reached, the entire body rots, dissolves and washes away. The chance of being infected by a character before death is not particularly high, but if you touch the green goo left behind by a character after death, there's a very high chance of being infected. 

## **[ Cotton Weaver ]** 

Symptoms=Cotton-like tissue peels off from the wound 

Rarity=14 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=1 week/5/10 (20) Fatal Depth=3 

Infection Power=Weak 

This is a strange disease that occurs due to a significant decrease in the power of undine within the body. The cause is a 

very small wound on the fingertip, etc. When infected with this illness, the wound will not close at all, but moreover, something that looks like cotton will start to bulge out of the wound. At _depth_ 1, the character's actions are not affected at all. If you pluck a little bit of the cotton, it will instantly disappear, but at 2, the character will fall into a coma and the cotton will come out no matter how much you pluck. Although the progression speed is not that fast, it is an incurable disease that is difficult to treat once contracted. 

## **[ Stone Skin ]** 

Symptoms=The surface of the body gradually becomes hard like stone 

Rarity=14 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=2 weeks/7/11 (22) Fatal Depth=4 Infection Power=Weak 

This is a terrifying illness in which the power of gnome becomes so strong that the surface of the body turns to stone beginning with the feet. At _depth_ 1, your ankles will become stiff and you won't be able to walk. At _depth_ 2, your knees become stiff. At _depth_ 3, your body will become stiff up to the waist, and at _depth_ 4, your chest will become stiff, at which point your character will be unable to breathe and will die of suffocation. However, the disease will continue to progress, and eventually your entire body will become a stone statue. 

## **[ Dance Macabre ]** 

Symptoms=The body keeps dancing separate from the person's will Rarity=13 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=Half day/6/9 (18) 

Fatal Depth=4 Infection Power=Strong 

When a person contracts this illness, their body begins to dance on its own, regardless of their consciousness. Since the person him or herself feels festive while suffering from the illness, it's believed that this is an abnormal 

manifestation of the power of leprechaun, which is a mind spirit. While suffering from this illness, you'll suffer a -4 penalty to your attack power and evasion speed, and you will not be able to cast ancient magic or spirit magic. As the _depth_ of the illness increases, the dancing becomes more intense. 

**[ Deep Sleep ]** Symptoms=Excessive sleep, extreme fatigue Rarity=11 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=3 days/6/9 (18) Fatal Depth=5 

Infection Power=Weak 

It's believed that this illness is caused by the power of the mind spirit sandman becoming too strong. ## **[ Lycanthropy ]** 

Symptoms=Become a lycanthrope 

Rarity=8 

Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score=Special/7/- (16) Fatal Depth=None Infection Power=Very weak 

This illness, which turns humans into lycanthropes, is thought to be different from normal illnesses caused by abnormalities in spirit power. Some sages claim that this is a type of curse (although these sages' claim is not accepted, as it cannot be removed with a _Remove Curse_ spell). 

Anyone wounded by a lycanthrope must roll 2D. If the roll is a 6 or less, you'll be infected with lycanthropy (the game master must secretly roll the dice if the adventurer does not know that lycanthropy is an infectious disease). 

The progression of lycanthropy differs from the rules of normal illnesses. Those infected with lycanthropy will have swollen wounds and a high fever. This fever continues endlessly. Every night from that day onwards, compare 2D + 3 with _the number of days since the new moon_ (if it's 16 days or more, then _30 - the number of days_ ). Once the score is equal to or less than the number of days, the character becomes a full lycanthrope and transforms into a beast form. This _cannot_ heal naturally. 

Before becoming a full lycanthrope, it can be cured with a spell such as the holy magic _Cure Disease_ , etc. (the target score is 16), but once you become a beast, you _cannot_ be cured again.

## **12.3 Work Requests to NPCs** 

You want a high-level sage to appraise the treasure you found during your adventure. You found a beautiful gemstone and would like to have it forged into a ring. As you progress through your adventures, such things will happen. 

In these cases, adventurers can request NPCs to do this kind of work. If it's a very simple task like those above, you can have it done for _NPC skill level x skill level x 30 gamels_ . For example, if you request an appraisal from a 5th level sage, you will have to pay 5 x 5 x 30 = **750** gamels. If the task becomes a little more complex, use numbers such as x _40_ or x _50_ instead of x 30. Also, if there's no one 

available to do this for you, there is of course no point in paying any amount of money. 

If the task you're requesting from an NPC is something that will completely restrain that NPC, you will need to **hire an NPC** . In this case, higher compensation is required. The average price per day is about _NPC's skill level multiplied by 50 to 200 gamels_ . 

Please follow the standards on Table 11-2: NPC Presence by Level (see p. 154) to find out which skill level NPCs are in which locations. Of course, this depends on the type of skill. Even in villages, there are likely to be people with a fairly high farmer skill level (although 

it's unlikely that you'll ask such people for anything). 

For requests for such work, you'd usually give 10% to 30% as an advance payment. If the work _fails_ , you do _not_ have to pay the remainder. If the contractor willfully abandons the job, it is customary to return double the advance payment to the client. 

This is the price an _adventurer_ must pay when making a request to an _NPC_ . Adventurers _themselves_ cannot go around charging these same rates. If you don't have clients, you won't get paid (so just patiently wait for work at the adventurer shop, okay?).
