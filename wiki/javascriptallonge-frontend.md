The front matter of *JavaScript Allongé, the "Six" Edition* (pages 1-13) introduces the book's focus on programming with functions, the reasons for the "Six" edition (ECMAScript 2015 updates), and its organizational structure. Key elements include:

- **Author**: Reg "raganwald" Braithwaite, who explains the book's pedagogical approach and its evolution with ECMAScript 2015.
- **Table of Contents**: Outlines chapters covering functional programming, data structures, iterators, and appendices.
- **Preface**: Discusses the book's emphasis on functional programming techniques, the impact of ECMAScript 2015 features (e.g., `let`, `const`, rest/spread syntax), and the transition from workarounds to native language support.
- **Foreword**: By Axel Rauschmayer, highlighting ECMAScript 2015's major features (classes, modules, generators) and how the book leverages them.
