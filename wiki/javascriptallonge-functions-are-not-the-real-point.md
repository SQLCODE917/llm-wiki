## Source record

Chapter on functions not being the real point in JavaScript Allongé, discussing the theoretical vs practical aspects of functional programming.

## Key supported claims

- Knowing how to make a linked list out of functions is not really necessary for the working programmer. (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.188-189)
- There are lots of similar texts explaining how to construct complex semantics out of functions. (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.188-189)
- Deeply important, but not practical when you're building a bridge. (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.188-189)
- Knowing how to make a list out of just functions is a little like knowing that photons are the Gauge Bosons 81 of the electromagnetic force. (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.188-189)
- Functions are a fundamental building block of computation. (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.188-189)
