### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 12.2 Rules for Poison, Illness and Infection / Intensity/ Healing Score 》

- After a certain period of time has elapsed after becoming ill, the illness either progresses further or gets better. The degree of progression of an illness is indicated by a score called **depth** . Immediately after contracting an illness, the _depth_ is 1. After the time indicated by **progression speed** has elapsed since you contracted the illness, roll two dice and look at the result. If the roll is less than the **progression intensity** , the disease gets worse and the _depth_ increases by 1. If the roll is greater than or equal to the _progression intensity_ but less than the **healing score** , the illness neither worsens nor improves, and the _depth_ remains the same. If the result is greater than or equal to the _healing score_ , the illness will be cured and the _depth_ will be reduced by 1. When the _depth_ becomes 0, you are completely recovered. ### Chapter 13: Monsters! / 13.2 Monster Catalog / 《 Life Points/Resistance 》

- **(Life point) resistance** is a substitute for a character's _life force resistance roll_ . When a character is poisoned, etc. a life force resistance roll determines whether they can avoid or reduce the effects of the poison. In the case of monsters, _life point resistance_ is used instead. When a monster is poisoned, etc. compare its resistance score with the strength of the poison (toxicity score).
