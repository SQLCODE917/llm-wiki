## Statements

- A spell that deceives the five senses of the target. These spells deceive the opponent's sensory organs and give them false sensations. There are three types of senses through which monsters perceive their surroundings: fi ve senses , pseudo senses, and magic senses. Magic of the illusion type is effective on those that perceive their surroundings with fi ve senses or pseudo senses. Humans and fae perceive their surroundings through fi ve senses , so they can all be targeted by illusions . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02700))_
- If the target does not have the senses to deceive, this spell will have no effect at all. It is completely ineffective against monsters who perceive their surroundings through magic senses, and illusions that deceive vision are useless against monsters who lack vision. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02701))_
- The effects of spells of the illusion type can be detected by a resistance roll. However, in order to do this, you must first be suspicious of the illusion. The game master should decide in which cases it seems suspicious. In the case of player characters (adventurers), this may be when the players themselves suspect an illusion. For NPCs and monsters, the game master should decide based on their intelligence. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02702))_
- For example, suppose a fake floor is created above a pit using a Create Image (ancient magic) spell. No matter how smart you are, you're not likely to notice this. But what if whoever was walking at the front of the line had the misfortune to step on top of it and fall? _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02703))_
- No matter how foolish you are, you should suspect that the floor is an illusion. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02704))_
- It might be unreasonable for a monster with low intelligence, such as a goblin, but a human of average intelligence would find it suspicious. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02706))_
- In the event of such a suspicion, the target of the illusion can make a mental power resistance roll. The target score is the final score from when the illusion spell was cast (if you forgot, roll the dice and determine it again). If you're successful, you'll be able to see through the illusion and know the correct situation. However, you can only make this resistance roll once . Once you fail, no matter how suspicious it may be, you won't be able to detect its true form (of course, you can walk around suspicious floors). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-02707))_
