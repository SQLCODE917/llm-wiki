## Source record

Thomas Paine's 'Document' section from '1776', pages 23-27, argues for independence from Britain and outlines the dangers of continued dependence.

## Key supported claims

- Paine argues that representation and election is too great a power for one body to possess, citing virtue is not hereditary (raw/1776ThomasPaine.pdf p.23-27).
- He notes that Britain's king's speech is a piece of villany, and that the king has broken moral and human obligations (raw/1776ThomasPaine.pdf p.23-27).
- Paine states that commerce, not conquest, is what benefits Britain from America (raw/1776ThomasPaine.pdf p.23-27).
- He argues that the king's speech is ill-judged and suited to his own interests, not the good of the nation (raw/1776ThomasPaine.pdf p.23-27).
- Paine concludes that nothing can settle affairs faster than an open declaration for independence, noting that France or Spain will not help if we aim to repair the breach (raw/1776ThomasPaine.pdf p.23-27).
