- The hunter skill is very similar to the ranger skill. Of course, the opposite is also true. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03418))_
- Therefore, a good ranger has the potential to be called a hero by the people, but a hunter, no matter how good he is, has a hard time getting people to recognize him as a hero. Of course, being a hunter is also a respectable profession, so you should never look down on it. People with higher levels in general skills should also be given due respect. However, they are not called heroes. They don't even become the protagonist of the story. They are unrelated to these _adventures_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03419))_
- Many of you may be thinking, _Then there's no need to separate the two, right?_ However, you can't do that. This is because the hunter skill and the ranger skill are completely different things. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03420))_
- The reason for this is that the hunter skill is a general skill, while the ranger skill is, as precisely called, an adventurer skill. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03422))_
- If you understand this mentality, then on the other hand, it would be acceptable to recognize adventurer skills for skills that would be called heroes. For example, the famous playwright William Tell, who was just a hunter, was considered a hero for shooting an apple off his child's head. In such cases, it is okay to grant adventurer skills to masters of general skills. However, basically in _Sword World RPG_ , only those who are masters of adventurer skills are worthy of being called heroes. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03423))_
