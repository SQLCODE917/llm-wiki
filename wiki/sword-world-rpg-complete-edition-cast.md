## Statements

### Chapter 3: Combat Rounds / 3.1 Action Announcements / Order of Action Announcements

- In an action announcement, you cannot declare something impossible. Casting a spell while running at full speed is prohibited by the rules. If there's an enemy in front of you that’s attacking you, attacking another enemy several meters away is not possible, just by common sense. These impossible actions cannot be declared, as a matter of course. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00674))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Procedure When a 5.1.4.5 Monster Casts Resistible Magic on an Adventurer

- When a monster casts magic, the procedure is different from when an adventurer casts magic. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01215))_

- Ducard II (mental power resistance 4) is attacked by a dark elf during an adventure. Dark elves have the ability to use spirit magic, with a magic intensity of 13. ### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Procedure When an 5.1.4.6 Adventurer Casts Resistible Magic on an Adventurer

- In other words, you compare the _magic power + 2D_ score of the one casting the magic with the _mental power resistance + 2D_ score of the one on whom the magic is being cast. If the score of the one casting the magic is greater, the magic will take _full_ effect, but if there is a tie or if the score of the one on whom the magic is being cast is greater, the magic is _resisted_ and will only take an _incomplete_ effect. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01231))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / When an Adventurer Casts Magic on Adventurers

- First, the side casting the magic rolls 2D. The final score (magic power + 2D) must then be determined this one time only. Each resisting side must then make a mental power resistance roll, using that determined final score as the target score. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01260))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / Example:

- Handling double ones and double sixes is no different from when there’s only one target. If the side casting the magic rolls double ones, the magic has _no_ effect on _any_ targets. If the side casting the magic rolls double sixes, _no_ _one_ can resist, except for those who _also_ roll double sixes. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01263))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / Adventurers and Monsters

- Sometimes enemies written in the same data format as a character may also be accompanied by monsters. For example, a sorcerer who leads undead monsters to attack villages. Casting magic on such a group is handled the same way as when an adventurer casts magic on (a group of) adventurers, as described above. At this time, the monsters must _also_ roll the dice to make resist checks. Please use the optional rules under _17.1: Changes to Magic Procedure_ (see p. 273). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01266))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.7 Magic Expansion / 《 Volume Expansion 》

- When you cast a spell by consuming that multiplied amount of mental power, multiply the number in _area expansion length_ or _volume expansion length_ by the number (radius, side) that represents the spell’s _area of effect_ , and the result will be the spell’s _area_ . For spells cast on a flat surface, use the number in the _area expansion length_ column, and for spells cast into space, use the number in the _volume expansion length_ column. For example, when you cast the ancient magic _Light_ , whose area is _A space with a 10 meter radius_ , by consuming _double_ the mental power, the resulting area will be a spherical space with a radius of _10 x 1.3 =_ _**13 meters**_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01314))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / Example:

- Suppose a sorcerer casts an _Energy Bolt_ on three enemies ( _x 3_ expansion) with a +1 to his final score ( _x 2_ expansion) and he makes 4 damage checks for each ( _x 4_ expansion). In this case, the multiplied rate of mental power consumed is 3 x 2 x 4 = **24!** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01336))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 《 Magic Power Decrease 》

- Magic power can be optionally reduced. This will reduce the final score and, if it's damage magic, _also_ reduce bonus damage (it’s not possible to _only_ reduce the final score or _only_ reduce bonus damage). You must declare how many points you’ll reduce _before_ you roll the dice. If used on multiple targets, you must reduce it by the same amount for _all_ of them. Also, if the side casting the magic rolls double sixes, the resisting side will _also_ need double sixes, no matter _how much_ magic power was declared to be decreased (if it's a monster, it _cannot_ resist). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01349))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.9 Magic Types / 《 Damage 》

- In either case, the caster's magic power can be added as bonus damage to the result of the strike roll. Also, the target's adventurer level (or the monster's monster level) is subtracted from the damage. However, there’s an _exception_ if you roll _double ones_ during your strike roll. In this case, no matter how much magic power your character (monster) has, they _cannot_ deal damage to the target at all. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01363))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.9 Magic Types / 《 Counters (Overpowers) 》

- The other, is when the prior magic has a _target score_ . In this case, according to the definition of _magic with a target score_ , the magic cast after will _not_ take effect unless its final score is greater than or equal to the determined _target score_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01381))_

- If the magic cast prior has a _target score_ and the magic cast after is _resistible_ , please follow the procedure below to make a check. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01382))_

- ② Next, the one casting the magic must roll 2D to find their final score (by adding their magic power). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01385))_

- Also, all magic _counters (overpowers)_ itself. Therefore, if certain magic takes effect, and you cast that same magic on the same target _multiple_ times, the effect will _not_ be cumulative. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01390))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.9 Magic Types / 《 Focus 》

- If the caster suffers damage to their life force or mental power, their focus will be interrupted and the magic's effect will be _lost_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01416))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.10 How to Read Magic Details / 《 Ritual 》

- With these spells, you can add +1 to your final score for each day you perform the ritual. If additional casters who can use the same spell also participate in the ritual, you will gain +1 to your final score for each one. Even humans who cannot use spells can participate in the ritual. In this case, you can gain +1 to your final score for every 10 people (rounded down). However, the ritual procedures are very complicated, so those who have not been taught the procedure in advance will not be able to participate in the ritual. If it's ancient magic, the caster must have been taught at a school such as an _academy of sages_ , etc. and if it's holy (dark) magic, they must be a believer. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01432))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.11 Ancient Magic and the Sorcerer Skill / 《 Distance 》

- This represents the distance the magic can reach. Basically, magic works on things that the caster can see and are within this distance. Any exceptions will always be noted in the explanation that follows. If the distance is **touch** , the caster must touch the target with their hand or (for ancient magic) with their magical catalyst (see below). To cast these spells in combat, you must be _engaged_ with the target. At this time, there is no need to roll dice to determine whether you've touched your opponent, like with an attack hit check (you can touch them automatically). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01456))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.12 Ancient Magic List / [ Hard Lock ]

Once cast, this spell's effect is permanent until it's removed. Also, no matter what material it's made of, this gives it the same strength as iron. ### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1.12 Ancient Magic List / [ Wraith Form ]

Wraiths are a kind of undead, but they can resume their life activities once they return to their own bodies. If you do not (or cannot) return to your original body after the spell's duration has expired, you will lose complete contact with your body, and must remain adrift for eternity as a wraith. The same applies if your body is destroyed (your life force or life points become 0 or less) while you are a wraith. If the wraith itself is destroyed, the caster dies.
