## Key supported claims

- The number of pennies needed to mark off 1 km can be estimated using the width of a penny, (raw/mit8_01scs22_chapter2.pdf p.30-36).
- The mass of water on Earth can be estimated using the density of water and the volume of oceans, (raw/mit8_01scs22_chapter2.pdf p.30-36).
- The probability that none of the molecules in your next breath were in Lao-Tzu's last breath is nearly zero, (raw/mit8_01scs22_chapter2.pdf p.30-36).
- The volume of the atmosphere can be estimated using a spherical shell model, (raw/mit8_01scs22_chapter2.pdf p.30-36).
- The volume of a breath can be converted to moles and then to the number of molecules using Avogadro's constant, (raw/mit8_01scs22_chapter2.pdf p.30-36).
