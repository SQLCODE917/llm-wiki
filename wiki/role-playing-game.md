# Role-Playing Game

A role-playing game (RPG) is a game where players act out roles within a fictional world, guided by a game master who facilitates the scenario. In this type of game, players take on the roles of characters and make decisions that affect the outcome of the game. The game master creates and manages the scenario, while the players make choices that influence how the story unfolds.

## Key Components

- **Players**: Individuals who take on the roles of characters within the game world.
- **Game Master (GM)**: The person who creates and facilitates the scenario, making decisions and interpreting rules during gameplay.
- **Characters**: The roles that players assume within the game world.
- **Scenario**: A prepared situation or story that the game master uses to guide the game.
- **Success Roll**: A dice roll used to determine if a character's action succeeds or fails.
- **Dice**: Six-sided dice used for randomization in gameplay, with 1D and 2D rolls common.

## Gameplay

In a role-playing game, players make decisions based on their character's abilities and characteristics. The game master provides the setting and context, but the players' choices determine how the story develops. The outcome of actions is often determined by rolling dice, which introduces an element of chance and uncertainty.

## Characteristics

- **Immersive Storytelling**: Players become immersed in the fictional world and make decisions that shape the narrative.
- **Collaborative Experience**: The game is a collaborative experience where players and the game master work together to create a story.
- **Randomness**: The use of dice introduces randomness, making outcomes uncertain and adding excitement to the game.
- **Character Development**: Players can develop their characters over time, improving abilities and skills through experience.

## Example

In the context of Sword World RPG, players might encounter a scenario where they are in a tavern and thugs start making remarks at a poster girl. The players must decide how to respond, and their choices will determine whether they get into a fight or not. The outcome of any conflict is determined by dice rolls, which introduces an element of chance.
