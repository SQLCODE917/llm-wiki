## 4.5 Attacks From Characters Against Monsters

When a character attacks a monster, the following checks are performed in order.

1. **Attack Check** – The character makes an attack check to see if the attack hits the monster.
2. **Damage Check** – If the attack hits, the character makes a damage check to determine how much damage the attack does.
3. **Defense Check** – If the attack hits, the monster makes a defense check to see if it can avoid the damage.
4. **Damage Calculation** – The final damage is calculated based on the damage check, defense check, and the character's attack points.

### Attack Check

The character makes an attack check. The target score for this check is the monster's defense points (see Section 4.4: Defense Points). If the check succeeds, the attack hits the monster. If the check fails, the attack misses the monster.

### Damage Check

If the attack hits the monster, the character makes a damage check. The target score for this check is the monster's defense points. If the check succeeds, the attack does damage to the monster. If the check fails, the attack does no damage to the monster.

### Defense Check

If the attack hits the monster, the monster makes a defense check. The target score for this check is the character's attack points. If the check succeeds, the monster avoids the damage. If the check fails, the monster takes the damage.

### Damage Calculation

If the attack hits the monster, the damage is calculated as follows:

1. The character's attack points are added to the damage check result.
2. The monster's defense points are subtracted from the total.
3. The result is the final damage dealt to the monster.

## Example

Let's say a character with 10 attack points attacks a monster with 5 defense points. The character makes an attack check and succeeds. The character then makes a damage check and gets a result of 8. The monster makes a defense check and fails. The damage is calculated as follows:

1. The character's attack points (10) are added to the damage check result (8), giving a total of 18.
2. The monster's defense points (5) are subtracted from the total (18), giving a final damage of 13.

This damage is then applied to the monster's life force (points).

## Attack Points

Attack points are a measure of how much damage a character can deal. They are determined by the character's weapon and the character's strength.

## Defense Points

Defense points are a measure of how well a monster can defend itself. They are determined by the monster's armor and the monster's agility.

## Life Force (Points)

Life force (points) is a measure of how much damage a character or monster can take before becoming unconscious or dying. It is determined by the character or monster's life force score.

## Unconscious and Death Checks

If a character's (monster's) life force (points) becomes _0 or negative_, that character (monster) will fall **unconscious**. It is no longer possible for them to act. If a character's (monster's) life force (points) becomes _negative_ and the absolute value of the life force (points) is greater than the character's (monster's) life force score, the character (monster) will **die**.

If a character or monster is unconscious, they can be revived by a successful **revival check**. If the check fails, the character or monster remains unconscious. If the check succeeds, the character or monster becomes conscious again and can act normally.

## Revival Check

A revival check is made when a character or monster is unconscious. The target score for this check is the character's or monster's life force score. If the check succeeds, the character or monster becomes conscious again and can act normally. If the check fails, the character or monster remains unconscious.
