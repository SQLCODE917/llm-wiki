## Statements

- If a roll comes up _double sixes_ or _double ones_ during combat between characters, the situation is resolved as follows: _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01006))_
- If the attacker's roll is _double ones_ , the attack _fails_ without question. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01007))_
- If the attacker's roll is _double sixes_ , it’s basically an automatic hit. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01008))_
- However, if the defender's roll is _also_ double sixes, the attack _fails_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01008))_
- However, if the attacker's roll is _also_ double ones, the attack _fails_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01009))_
- If the defender's roll is _double ones_ , it’s basically an automatic hit. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01009))_
- It doesn’t matter even if the attacker rolls double sixes. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01010))_
- If the defender's roll is _double sixes_ , the attack will _always_ fail. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01010))_
