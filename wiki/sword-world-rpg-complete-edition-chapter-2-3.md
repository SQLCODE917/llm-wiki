## Chapter 2.3: Checks

In _Sword World RPG_, actions are resolved through a series of checks. These checks involve determining a character's baseline score and the target score for the action, then rolling dice to see if the action succeeds.

### Baseline Scores

A **baseline score** is a number that indicates how competent or skilled a character is at a given activity. It is calculated as:

**baseline score** = relevant skill level + ability bonus

For example, if a character has a fighter skill level of 2 and a dexterity bonus of +2, their baseline score for fighting is 4.

Skills are categorized into combat and magic skills, which are useful primarily in combat or magic, and other skills that are used in a variety of situations (see Chapter 6: Adventurer).

If a character does not have any skills related to an action, the baseline score is 0 regardless of their ability scores.

### Target Scores

The **target score** represents the difficulty of an action. It is determined by the game master based on factors such as the opponent's experience or the complexity of the task.

Target scores for certain actions can be found in _Part 2: Game Master Section_.

### Checks

Once the baseline score and target score are determined, a success roll **check** can finally be made.

**final score** = baseline score + 2D

If the final score is **equal to or greater than** the target score, the action is successful; otherwise, it fails.

### Automatic Success and Automatic Failure

Even the most difficult acts can succeed by a quirk of fate. Conversely, no matter how simple something may seem, it may fail due to some misfortune.

- **Automatic Success**: If a player rolls double sixes (12), the action is a success, no matter how low the baseline score or how high the target score.
- **Automatic Failure**: If a player rolls double ones (2), the action fails, no matter how high the baseline score or how low the target score.

### Bonuses and Penalties

Success rolls may be subject to modifiers, representing favorable or unfavorable circumstances. These are applied to the baseline score before rolling.

For example:
- A +2 bonus for fighting someone who is falling
- A -4 penalty for fighting in the dark

### Difficulty Checks

Some actions are not known to succeed or fail by the character. These are called **difficulty checks**. In these cases, the game master rolls dice (2D) and adds them to the difficulty score to determine the target score. The players do not know the game master's roll or the resulting target score.

- If the player's final score is **greater than or equal to** the target score (difficulty + 2D), the action succeeds.
- If the player's final score is **less than** the target score, the action fails.

The game master does not reveal whether the action succeeded or failed, only what the character can tell from their success or failure.

### Success Rolls Using Adventurer Level

For actions that anyone would be able to perform if they became proficient in adventuring, the baseline score is calculated as:

**baseline score** = adventurer level + ability bonus

Actions that fit into this category are explained in _6.6: Common Adventurer Abilities_.

### Resistance Rolls

A **resistance roll** is a special type of check that uses adventurer level in its baseline score calculation.

- **Life Force Resistance Roll**: Uses _adventurer level + life force bonus_
- **Mental Power Resistance Roll**: Uses _adventurer level + mental power bonus_

These rolls are used to resist physical dangers (like poison) or magical effects (like curses or spells).
