Illusion magic is a type of magic in the Sword World RPG that creates false sensations to deceive opponents. It is effective against those who perceive their surroundings through five senses or pseudo senses, but ineffective against monsters who perceive through magic senses.

## Types of Senses

There are three types of senses through which monsters perceive their surroundings:

1. **Five senses**: Humans and fae perceive their surroundings through five senses, so they can all be targeted by illusions.
2. **Pseudo senses**: These are additional sensory capabilities that some creatures possess.
3. **Magic senses**: Monsters who perceive through magic senses are immune to illusions that deceive vision.

## Effectiveness

Illusion magic works on targets that have five senses or pseudo senses. It is completely ineffective against monsters who perceive their surroundings through magic senses. Illusions that deceive vision are useless against monsters who lack vision.

## Detection

The effects of spells of the illusion type can be detected by a resistance roll. However, in order to do this, you must first be suspicious of the illusion. The game master should decide in which cases it seems suspicious.

For example, suppose a fake floor is created above a pit using a _Create Image_ (ancient magic) spell. No matter how smart you are, you're not likely to notice this. But what if whoever was walking at the front of the line had the misfortune to step on top of it and fall? No matter how foolish you are, you should suspect that the floor is an illusion.

Or, what if you were in a hallway with a thick layer of dust, but only one part of it was free of dust? It might be unreasonable for a monster with low intelligence, such as a goblin, but a human of average intelligence would find it suspicious.

In the event of such a suspicion, the target of the illusion can make a mental power resistance roll. The target score is the final score from when the illusion spell was cast (if you forgot, roll the dice and determine it again). If you're successful, you'll be able to see through the illusion and know the correct situation. However, you can only make this resistance roll once. Once you fail, no matter how suspicious it may be, you won't be able to detect its true form (of course, you can walk around suspicious floors).
