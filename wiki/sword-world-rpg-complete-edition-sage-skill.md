The sage skill represents a comprehensive understanding and depth of knowledge in all academic fields. Academic fields in _Sword World_ are undifferentiated, and owners of this skill are skilled in all disciplines. Many sorcerers have this skill. There is no requirement that you have to have the sage skill and the sorcerer skill at the same time, but if you _do_ acquire the sage skill and the sorcerer skill at the same time, you will receive a _bonus_ on experience points consumed. For more information, please refer to Chapter 8: Character Growth (see p. 129).

The sage skill includes the following abilities:

- ① Monster Check
- ② Languages
- ③ Astrology
- ④ Knowledge
- ⑤ Research
- ⑥ Treasure Appraisal
- ⑦ Medicines

## ① Monster Check [Intelligence] (Not retriable)

Sages have a wide range of knowledge. This includes knowledge of the various monsters that live in Alecrast. When a sage sees a monster or hears its name, this ability may allow them to discover its true identity.

Each monster has a score called _rarity_ . Using _sage skill level + intelligence bonus_ as the baseline score, make a success roll with this rarity as the target score, and if you succeed, you know the monster. If the check fails, the character has no knowledge of the monster. Even if the _player_ knows, they must treat it as if they had no prior knowledge.

## ② Languages [Intelligence]

Sages can learn different languages depending on their skill level. Each time their sage skill level increases, a character can learn to speak or read _one_ new language. The player chooses which language to learn, along with whether they will _speak_ or _read_ it. However, _runes_ cannot be chosen. Even in the same language, being able to read and speak is different, so if you want to become proficient in _one_ language, you need _2_ levels of experience. You can also _write_ in any language that you can read and speak.

There are limitations to learning languages through the sage skill. Characters who cannot read common must _first_ learn how to read common _before_ learning other languages. Then, a character who cannot read low ancient (but _has_ learned common) must learn to read low ancient _before_ learning any other language. If you can read both common _and_ low ancient, you are free to learn subsequent languages.

## ③ Astrology [Intelligence] (Not retriable)

From the movement of celestial bodies, you can know the signs of events that will occur in the near future. Signs should be given as rough information about the incident. It's enough to let you know that a king passes away or that a major war is about to happen (you can think of it as just a headline in a newspaper, etc.). However, this ability _only_ allows you to know about events related to the country or the world, and when _no_ events occur, _no_ signs appear. It is entirely up to the game master to decide what kind of sign there is. You can also set a difficulty and ask for a success roll. Solar and lunar eclipses rarely occur in Forcelia, but when they do, they are always a harbinger of something to come.

## ④ Knowledge [Intelligence] (Not retriable)

The scope of this ability is very wide. It's for checks regarding whether you know or don't know about things in all fields, from trivia to politics to science. There are two methods of making checks. One is one where the game master judges whether or not the player knows based on their sage skill level and tells them, without providing a _rarity_ . The other is to provide a _rarity_ for each piece of knowledge, and ask for a check via success roll, similar to _monster check_ . Please use whichever method the game master deems appropriate for each situation.

## ⑤ Research [Intelligence]

When a sage fails a success roll on a check such as _monster check_ , _treasure appraisal_ (described below), or _knowledge_ and is unable to figure it out, the sage can later find out by **researching** literature.

Research basically takes about one week for each item (this may be increased or decreased at the game master's discretion). During this time, you cannot do much other than research. Also, if you don't have a place with a wealth of literature (the best place is the academy of sages = sorcerers' guild in each city), research itself is impossible. If the research is successful, the sage may make another success roll. When something they didn't know (something that failed) becomes a success, the sage has found a description of it in the literature and has access to that knowledge.

## ⑥ Treasure Appraisal [Intelligence] (Not retriable)

This ability is used to know the value and utility of treasure. The _rarity_ of that treasure becomes the target score for a success roll. For standard ancient kingdom treasure-- many of which are magical items --the target score is around 15. Treat a failed treasure as being unable to _determine_ the treasure's value, _not_ as if you got the value _wrong_ . If the true value is 100 gamels, you will _not_ think that it's worth 10,000 gamels just because you failed the check. However, if the difference from the target score is 6 or greater, there _is_ a possibility of this kind of misunderstanding.

The price value determined by this ability is the price that a connoisseur (or obsessor) would accept, i.e., the _maximum_ price. Alternatively, if it is something that can be used in combat, such as weapons or armor, other adventurers may purchase it. Also, any item can be picked up by a specialty shop for half the appraised value. Basically, these connoisseurs and specialty shops will only exist in large towns.

## ⑦ Medicines [Intelligence] ((Appraisal only) Not retriable)

This ability represents knowledge of poisons and medicines. Sages can appraise unknown medicines and poisons, and can mix magical potions and poisons with various effects.

When appraising medicines or poisons, a success roll must be made using their rarity as the target score. To create medicines or poisons, the game master should set a target score depending on the type, and ask for a success roll check. The target score for a poison which is able to kill people even in small amounts _and_ be tasteless and odorless, is around 20. Regardless of the result or dice roll, you will need materials and at least one week to attempt to create a medicine. You can make up to 10 doses at most.

In Alecrast, the creation and use of poison is considered _shameful_ and _inhumane_ . Therefore, game masters should _not_ allow PCs to create or use poisons. For detailed rules regarding poison, please also refer to 12.2.1: Poisons (see p. 159).
