Animal monsters in the Sword World RPG - Complete Edition are a category of creatures that are similar to animals in our world but with varying sizes and abilities. They are typically found in various habitats and can pose different levels of threat to adventurers. These monsters are categorized by their monster level, rarity, agility, movement speed, and other characteristics.

## Characteristics

Animal monsters are generally classified based on their monster level, which corresponds to a character's adventurer level and indicates the general strength of the monster. They have specific attributes such as agility, movement speed, number, frequency, intellect, reaction, attack points, strike points, evasion points, defense points, life points/resistance, mental points/resistance, special abilities, habitat, languages, and perception.

## Behaviors

Animal monsters usually act according to their instincts and environmental conditions. For example, wolves are carnivorous and usually act in packs, while giant rats are intelligent and dexterous, and can stand on their hind legs and use simple tools. Some animal monsters, like the hanger leg, are parasitic and can take over the bodies of large animals. Others, like eagles, are aerial hunters that attack from above, and killer sharks are aquatic predators that attack anything that moves in the sea.

## Roles in the Game

Animal monsters serve as a source of danger and challenge for adventurers. They can be encountered in various locations such as forests, caves, jungles, deserts, and seas. Their rarity and monster level determine how often they are encountered and how difficult they are to defeat. Some animal monsters, like wardogs, are trained for combat and can be used by adventurers in battle. Others, like vampire bats, are bloodsucking creatures that can pose a threat to adventurers.

## Key Concepts

- **Monster Level**: A score that corresponds to a character's adventurer level, representing the general strength of that monster.
- **Rarity**: A score that indicates whether the monster is famous or not.
- **Agility**: Similar to agility, which is a character ability score, this represents the quickness of a monster.
- **Movement Speed**: Indicates the speed at which the monster moves.
- **Special Abilities**: Unique abilities the monster possesses that may affect combat and magic interactions.
- **Habitat**: The environment where the monster is typically found.
- **Perception**: The monster's sensory abilities.
- **Languages**: The languages the monster can speak.

## Combat and Magic

Animal monsters have specific combat rules and magic interactions. For example, when an adventurer attacks an eagle, they will suffer a -2 penalty to their attack because it's airborne. When a vampire bat attaches itself to its opponent, it will begin bloodsucking starting on the next round. These interactions are detailed in the monster's data sheet.

## Experience Points

Animal monsters provide experience points to adventurers when defeated, contributing to character growth and skill development.

Some animal monsters have special abilities that may affect combat and magic interactions, such as disease, poison, and paralysis. These are detailed in the monster's data sheet.
