# **NPC and Monster Action Announcements**

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about **npc and monster action announcements**:

## Statements

- When you see it for the first time, you won't know how a monster you don't recognize will act, and when an enemy tries to cast a spell you don't recognize, you won't know what that spell does. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00690))_
- Specific action announcements may not be made at this time. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00686))_
- This is the difference in position between the players and the game master. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00690))_
- The game master, as the person who manages the game, must know the actions of all the characters and monsters that appear. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00690))_
- The game master must manage the actions of NPCs and monsters. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03646))_
- In combat, the planned actions of NPCs and monsters must be announced at the beginning of the round. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03646))_
- At this time, the game master does not need to make a specific announcement. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03647))_
- However, that does _not_ mean that you can make loose announcements and change them depending on the situation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03648))_
- However, the game master should decide in advance which actions they are going to take. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03649))_
- This can lead to a loss of game fairness and may also spoil player motivation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03650))_
- If those are _also_ the same, then compare the _third_ highest. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00685))_
- If the two groups are _equal_ in intelligence (i.e., the highest intelligence in each group is the same), compare the _second_ highest in each group. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00685))_
- However, the players should only act from their _characters’_ point of view. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00690))_
- So, if a _character_ cannot predict something, the _player_ cannot know it either. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00690))_
- Of course, if a character has a wealth of knowledge, or if they’re an advanced spellcaster, they _might_ know such things. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00691))_
- All they have to do is tell you what you can understand from the character's perspective. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03647))_

## Technical atoms

### Technical atom 1

**Context:** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00686))_

> When NPCs, monsters, etc. announce their actions, it’s done by the game master who manages them. Specific action announcements may not be made at this time.

**Atom:** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00687))_

> If a settlement cannot be reached, the game master gets to announce their group's actions first (NPCs and monsters) and then the players follow.

**Context:** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00688))_

**Context:** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03649))_
