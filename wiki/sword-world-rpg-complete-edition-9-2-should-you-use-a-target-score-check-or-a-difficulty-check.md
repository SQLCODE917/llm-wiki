## Source record

This section explains when to use target score checks versus difficulty checks, detailing how to decide based on whether outcomes are obvious and outlining how to keep difficulty hidden when it would reveal hidden information.

## Key supported claims

- The core problem for success rolls is deciding between a target score or a difficulty. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138)
- Success rolls require selecting the skill, relevant ability score, and either target score or difficulty. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138)
- When difficulty is clear, a target score should be used; otherwise, a difficulty check is appropriate. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138)
- Traps with varying difficulties can be presented as a difficulty check without revealing the exact difficulty. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138)
- A master character is unlikely to fail, but a pro can fail about 50‑50; failure probability may vary with skill level. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138)
