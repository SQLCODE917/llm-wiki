# **Starting Experience Points**

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about **starting experience points**:

## Statements

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / 1.4.3 Starting Skills and Starting Experience Points

- Even an adventurer who goes on an adventure for the first time is not a baby. As long as they've lived a certain number of years, they must have had some experience before entering the life of an adventurer. Once ability scores have been determined, the next step is to determine the adventurer's preadventure experience. This is represented by **starting skills** and **starting experience points** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00340))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / For Races Other Than Humans

- For races other than human (except for half-elves raised by humans), the starting skills and experience points are determined by race (Table 1-6: Starting Experience by Race). **Example:** _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00350))_

- Since the character we're creating is a human, we'll use the _backgrounds_ table. Our roll is "9". The character ended up coming from the _mercenary_ background. Therefore, we'll have 1 level in the fighter skill as our starting skill, and 2500 starting experience points. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00352))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / Character Growth via

- In addition, starting experience points do not have to be used up. Leaving them in place will give you an advantage in the growth of your character after the game. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00387))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / Starting Experience Points

- A character has already acquired certain skills (starting skills) according to Table 1-5: Backgrounds or Table 1-6: Starting Experience by Race. In addition to this, starting experience points can also be used to acquire new skills or increase a character's skills. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00389))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / Example:

- Characters can spend their starting experience points to gain skill levels. For example, if you want to acquire 1 level in ranger, you can do so by subtracting 500 from your starting experience points. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00395))_

- As this shows, the use of starting experience points is exactly the same as the use of experience points during character growth, so it might be a good idea to read the explanation in chapter 8 first. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00403))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / [ ⑨ Money ]

- Table 1-5: Backgrounds or Table 1-6: Starting Experience by Race determine the amount of money (2D x _modifier_ ) that you have. Roll the dice, calculate it, and write it down in the _money_ field. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00509))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / [ ⑭ Adventurer Level ]

- Once you’ve acquired your final skills (starting skill and growth via starting experience points), you can determine your adventurer level. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00521))_

### Chapter 13: Monsters! / 13.2 Monster Catalog / 13.2.6 Mythical Beasts and Magical Beasts / Monster Level=4

- In combat, an androscorpio uses a sword in his hand, but he can also attack with his tail at the same time. The stinger at the tip of his tail contains a hallucinogenic poison (toxicity score 11). Those wounded by a tail attack and fail a life force resistance roll will experience hallucinations starting on the next round. The enemy's appearance can change, or it can appear as multiple . This symptom lasts for one hour, during which time the victim suffers a -4 penalty on all success rolls. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04986))_
