## Source record

This section details rules for determining treasure and reward amounts in the Sword World RPG, based on adventurer levels and scenario difficulty.

## Key supported claims

- Adventurers search for treasure in ruins where silver and gold coins did not exist, finding handicrafts like gems, precious metals, and works of art. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241-241)
- Magic items such as weapons, armor, books, and scrolls imbued with ancient magic are extremely valuable and cannot be appraised without specialized knowledge. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241-241)
- Rewards for resolving jobs in towns and villages are usually in silver coins (gamels), though they can also be in the form of a kiss from a beautiful maiden or special knowledge. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241-241)
- To determine the value (upper limit) of treasure obtainable in silver coins, multiply the total adventurer levels of all player characters by 500. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241-241)
- Rewards should be equal per person regardless of individual level differences. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241-241)

## Technical details

### `technical-atom-46f05d50ac6d94ca` procedure

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241)

Typical adventurers in Alecrast use one adventurer’s shop as their base for adventure, thereupon gathering information about the ruins of the ancient kingdom, then setting off after readying their equipment, making their living selling the spoils.

### `technical-atom-6414896e75d8784c` procedure

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.241)

To determine the value (upper limit) of the treasure that can be obtained in terms of silver coins, multiply the adventurer levels of all the player characters, then multiply the total by 500.
