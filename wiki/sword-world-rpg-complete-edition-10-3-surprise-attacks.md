## Source record

This section outlines how surprise attacks are determined and the relevant tables for attack and evasion checks.

## Key supported claims

- Table 10‑1 lists attack power and evasion speed modifiers for conditions such as full movement and visibility issues (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-142).
- Surprise attack checks are determined by the game master using Table 10‑2; the master rolls 2D and adds the monster level (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-142).
- Attacks against sleeping monsters are always a success unless a 2D roll results in double ones on the hit check (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-142).
- When a monster is not noticed, a player may perform a surprise attack (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-142).
- Even if surprised, the side may still try to avoid attacks or resist magic; such actions may be taken (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-142).
