### 【 Shaman 】

The spirit world is the world of spirits who command various powers. ### [ Suppress Elemental ]

- This spell has the effect of negating any one of the four major spirit powers of earth, water, fire, or wind. However, only one kind of spirit power can be erased at a time. This spell cannot be expanded to block two or more kinds of spirit power from working at the same time. As a result of this, spirit magic that depends on that spirit power will no longer be usable. Also, if a spell effect is present due to the negated spirit power (either gnome, undine, salamander, or sylph) within the spell's area of effect, it will be negated. If that spirit is present, it will also disappear from existence and return to the spirit world. However, this has no effect on greater spirits (behemoth, kraken, efreet, djinn). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-01819))_

### ⬤ Wind Spirits

- It must be in a place where the wind blows in its natural state. The spirit power of wind does not work inside buildings, deep caves, or even underground passages. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02266))_

### ⬤ Earth Spirits

- This is a power that's at work in places where the ground is exposed or underground. Particularly strong spirit power is at work in the mountains. Since the power of water spirits is strong on the ocean floor and the bottoms of lakes, the spirit power of earth does not work there. Likewise, this spirit power does not work on the floors of buildings (whether they're stone or wood). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02268))_

### ⬤ Water Spirits

- The spirit power of water is at work wherever there is water, such as the ocean or a lake. Even if you carry water in a water bottle, etc., if you open the lid, the spirit power will work. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02270))_

### ⬤ Fire Spirits

- This spirit power is at work wherever there's fire burning. You can also use fire from a torch you're carrying with you. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02272))_

### ⬤ Ice Spirits

- This spirit power is at work wherever there is snow or ice. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02274))_

### ⬤ Mind Spirits

- This spirit power is at work wherever there are living things with functioning minds. Of course if the caster him or herself is like that, there's no problem. Some of these types of spirits include those that control the nature of the mind, and those that control fluctuation of emotions. It's said that if one emotion has too much of an outburst, it can cause phenomena such as alienation or complete loss of functions of other spirits, which can lead to mental disorders. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02276))_

### 《 Sense Aura 》

- Sense aura is a subtle sensation, more like what we can smell than see . Even if you know that spirit power is at work, you won't be able to tell where that spirit power is coming from unless you get very close. Also, if there's extremely strong spirit power in your surroundings, you may not be able to sense weak spirit power at work. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02289))_

### 《 Silent Spirit 》

- These are runes used to communicate with spirits. Those who can use these runes can communicate with any spirit. Most spirits speak directly to the mind without using words, but when a caster gives commands to a spirit, they must speak these runes out loud. Also, some fae are well versed in silent spirit. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02291))_

### [ Shade ] (Shade/Dark Spirit)

- Expansion=Final score, duration, distance, targets, damage certainty Resist=Reduced effect Shade is the spirit of darkness that opposes will-o-wisp, and is also the spirit that controls fear. It is said to have a spherical shape like the will-o-wisp, but this is not certain (because like a crow in the pitch-black night, it cannot be seen). All natural light within a 5 meter radius of this spirit is negated, closing it in complete darkness. The darkness created by a shade has no effect in a space where the ancient magic Light is at work. In addition, if the light emitted by a will-owisp and the darkness produced by a shade overlap, the powers of both will be negated. The shade will fly freely in the air according to the caster's commands, but can no longer be controlled if it moves more than 20 meters away from the caster. This has no physical effect, but it impairs mental activity and has the effect of reducing mental power (points). Make a strike power 10 damage check, and subtract the result from mental power (points). The damage is only dealt to mental power (points), the rest of the check is the same as for magic that deals normal damage. If mental power (points) becomes 0 or negative, the target loses consciousness. Furthermore, if an opponent destroys a shade with a weapon they're holding, the one who took that action will also suffer the same damage. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02391))_

### [ Control Spirit ] (Various lesser spirits)

A shaman can only control one kind of spirit at a time. A controlled spirit can communicate with its caster using silent spirit, and will carry out simple commands, but cannot do much. It cannot attack others directly. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02463))_

- The biggest advantage of controlling spirits is that you can cast spirit magic that belongs to them, even in places where spirit power would not normally be at work. It's always possible to release a controlled spirit. A controlled spirit can only be used by its controller. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02464))_

- The main spirits that can be controlled are gnome (earth spirits), undine (water spirits), salamander (fire spirits), sylph (wind spirits), brownie (building spirits), leprechaun (mind spirits), sprite (mind spirits), etc. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-02465))_
