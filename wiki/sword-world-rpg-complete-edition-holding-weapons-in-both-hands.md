## Holding Weapons in Both Hands

This concept refers to extended rules in the *Sword World RPG - Complete Edition* for characters who hold weapons in both hands, detailing attack options, penalties, and special cases for ambidextrous characters.

### Key Aspects

-   **Attack Options**: A character with weapons in both hands has three options for attacking:
    1.  **Attack with the weapon in your dominant hand** = Same as basic rules.
    2.  **Attack with the weapon in your opposite hand** = -2 penalty to attack power.
    3.  **Attack with both hands at the same time** = -2 penalty to attack with _dominant_ hand, _-4_ penalty to attack with _opposite_ hand. Also, the required strength for _one_ of the weapons is limited to _half your own strength (rounded up)_. If you're fighting with the thief skill, the required strength for _both_ weapons is only _half_ the character's strength. There's _no_ need to reduce one to a quarter.
-   **Ambidextrous Characters**: When a character is ambidextrous, there is _no_ opposite hand. If you attack with only _one_ hand, there is _no_ penalty, and if you attack with _both_ hands, the penalty is only _-2_ for each.
-   **Dominant Hand**: Characters are generally righthanded or left-handed, but in actuality there are also people who are _ambidextrous_. Therefore, when creating an adventurer, let's say that if you roll 2D and get _double sixes_ , they'll be ambidextrous. Left-handed or right-handedness is decided by the player's preference.
