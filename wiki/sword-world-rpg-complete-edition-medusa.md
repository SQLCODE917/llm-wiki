A medusa is a magical beast that takes the form of a human woman, with dozens of snakes growing out of her head instead of hair, and a face with the magical power to petrify humans. They dwell mainly in ruins and underground labyrinths, and find wicked pleasure in turning visitors into stone statues. Anyone who encounters a medusa must succeed on a mental power resistance roll against target score 10 or they will instantly be turned into a stone statue (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.188-198).

## Behavior

Medusas are known for their petrifying gaze, which turns anyone who looks at them into stone. To avoid being petrified, one must either fight with their eyes closed or face turned away, which incurs a -4 penalty to attack power and evasion speed. Alternatively, one can use a mirror to fight while looking at the medusa's reflection, which reduces the penalty to -2. Medusas use the snakes on their head to attack, and anyone who takes damage from these attacks must make a life force resistance roll against target score 10. If they fail, they suffer damage from a strike power 10 strike roll plus bonus damage 3 (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.188-198).

## Notes

Medusas are part of the magical beasts category in the Sword World RPG - Complete Edition (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.188-198). Even after death, the magical power remains in their head for a while, allowing the severed head to be used as a weapon to turn enemies to stone (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.188-198).
