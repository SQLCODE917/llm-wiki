## Source record

Foreword to the 'Six' edition of JavaScript Allongé, which provides historical context for the development of ECMAScript 6.

## Key supported claims

- ECMAScript 6 was ratified as a standard on June 17, and its origins date back to the year 2000 (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.13-13).
- TC39 started work on ECMAScript 4, which was planned to have numerous new features, turning JavaScript into a completely new language (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.13-13).
- After internal conflict, a settlement was reached in July 2008, abandoning ECMAScript 4 and replacing it with two upgrades, a smaller and a larger (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.13-13).
