Magic contraction in Sword World RPG allows rune masters to reduce the area, volume, duration, or power of magic effects, but does not reduce the amount of mental power consumed.

## Contraction Types

Magic contractions include the following:
- Area Contraction
- Volume Contraction
- Duration Contraction
- Magic Power Decrease
- Effect Contraction

## Combined Expansion and Contraction

Although it's not often done, it's possible to perform magic expansion and contraction at the same time. For example, you could extend the area of effect while reducing magic power. The mental power required at this time is decided by expansion. It cannot be reduced by contraction. For example, if you double the area and reduce magic power by -4, the mental power consumed will still be double.

## Area Contraction

You can make the area of effect narrower than originally intended. The shape (usually a circle) cannot be changed. You also cannot cast it in such a way that it hits the enemy directly in the eyes.

## Volume Contraction

Similar to area contraction, you can also reduce the volume of the targeted object or space. Again, the shape cannot be changed, and you cannot directly aim it at the enemy's eyes.

## Duration Contraction

Magic with a duration can be optionally shortened. However, you cannot set a time that causes the effect to wear off in the middle of a round. You may only set it to wear off at the end of a round. You must also decide how long you want the magic to last before you cast it. Magic that's already been cast cannot be canceled at will.

## Magic Power Decrease

Magic power can be optionally reduced. This will reduce the final score and, if it's damage magic, also reduce bonus damage (it's not possible to only reduce the final score or only reduce bonus damage). You must declare how many points you'll reduce before you roll the dice. If used on multiple targets, you must reduce it by the same amount for all of them. Also, if the side casting the magic rolls double sixes, the resisting side will also need double sixes, no matter how much magic power was declared to be decreased (if it's a monster, it cannot resist).

## Effect Contraction

All magic that can expand their effects can also contract their effects.
