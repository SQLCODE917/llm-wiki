# AoE2 Opening Interactions

Opening interactions in Age of Empires II refer to how players respond to each other during the early stages of a match, particularly in terms of resource gathering and unit deployment.

## Source-backed details

| Claim | Evidence | Locator | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening interactions determine the early game advantage and set the tone for the match. | "In isolation, opening interactions, army composition planning, game plans are theoretical questions which might have definitive answers to them, but introducing actual gameplay into the mix makes everything a big old \"it depends\" problem." | `normalized:L563` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Drush is a safe opening that aims to slow down the enemy. | "Drush is for all purposes an extremely safe opening which has simple and easy to achieve goals of slowing down the enemy." | `normalized:L503` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Mongols have fast openings with faster hunt gathering. | "Mongols obviously have very fast openings with their faster hunt gathering, so they will be hitting with something like low population, scouts/maa/archer build." | `normalized:L533` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Openings have follow-ups and can transition into different unit types. | "Obviously openings have follow ups and you can transition into archers or skirms out of drush or maa, or you can race to castle age." | `normalized:L201` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
