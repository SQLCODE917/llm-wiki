# AoE2 Military Strategy

Military strategy in Age of Empires II encompasses the principles of unit combat effectiveness, force multiplication, and tactical positioning that determine success in battles.

## Source-backed details

| Claim | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Lanchester's Linear Law applies to one-on-one duels where fighting strength is proportional to group size | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_9318c84e] |
| Lanchester's Square Law applies to all-against-all combat where fighting strength is proportional to the square of group size | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_8d7acc24] |
| Combat outcomes can escalate dramatically with small numerical advantages, creating snowball effects in battles | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_e2116061] |
| Large numerical advantages can overcome unit type disadvantages in combat | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_fe84b9de] |
| Tactical positioning and terrain advantages can compensate for numerical disadvantages | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_bf0e7e01] |
| Terrain features like hills provide significant combat advantages in AoE2 | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_b9ba9f56] |
| When facing a numerically superior enemy on unfavorable terrain, targeting their economy is a viable strategy | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_a39b3feb] |
| Timing and positioning of military actions can exploit temporary advantages from unit upgrades | [aoe2-basics:claim_aoe2-basics_c003_5ba86800] |
