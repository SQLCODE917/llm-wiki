# AoE2 Lanchester's Law

Lanchester's Law is a mathematical model that describes combat effectiveness in military contexts, which applies to unit interactions in Age of Empires II.

## Source-backed details

| Claim | Evidence | Locator | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lanchester's Linear Law states that combat strength is proportional to group size in one-on-one duels. | "Lanchester's Linear Law states that, **where combat between two groups is a series of one-on-one duels, fighting strength is proportional to group size**, as one would expect." | `normalized:L338` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Lanchester's Square Law states that combat strength is proportional to the square of group size in all-against-all combat. | "However, Lanchester's Square Law states that, **where combat is all-against-all, fighting strength is proportional to the square of group size**." | `normalized:L338` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Bloodlines are not worth getting unless you have a large number of scouts. | "Lanchesters law is strong here too and it's actually not worth getting bloodlines unless you have at least 6-7 scouts out in the field." | `normalized:L411` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
