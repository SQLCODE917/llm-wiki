Withdrawal from engagement refers to the action of fleeing from an opponent within melee attack range. In Sword World RPG, this action has specific rules and potential penalties.

When a character attempts to withdraw (flee) from engagement, they can escape from an opponent within melee attack range. However, if an attack is declared on a character who is withdrawing, another character is needed to suffer the enemy's attack in their place (defend against the attack).

If a character does not have a companion to take on the opponent, they will escape with their back completely turned, and will suffer a **-4 penalty** when evading the attack. Additionally, regardless of the character's agility, they will only escape at the _end_ of the round (agility 0, based on action order). This means there is always a chance for the enemy to attack the character before they can fully withdraw.

Even with high agility, a character can still attack during the withdrawal attempt, and they will have a higher chance of hitting than usual.

This rule reflects the tactical complexity of combat, where attempting to flee from an engaged opponent is both dangerous and potentially disadvantageous.
