### Chapter 12: Various Checks / Rules for Poison, Illness and Infection

- In _Sword World RPG_ , illness is defined as a state in which the balance of spirit power within the body is disrupted. The concept of bacterial or viral contagion does not exist. There are two types of symptoms that can appear. One is due to **poison** and the other is due to **illness** . In addition, a wound may become **infected** , which is treated as an illness. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04260))_
- Poisons force an imbalance in the spirit power within the body. As long as the poison remains in the body, even if the symptoms disappear, the balance of spirit power will be disrupted and the symptoms will appear again. However, if the poison itself is cleared away, the spirit power within the body will naturally return to a balanced state and restore health. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04261))_
- On the other hand, illness is a state where the spirit power within the body remains unbalanced and in a fixed state. Think of it as the spirits inside your body that are under the illusion that a false equilibrium is the correct one. Illness infection occurs when spirits transmit information about the incorrect equilibrium state to spirits in other human bodies. The unknown spirit of life heals illness by showing other spirits in the body how to achieve balance. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04262))_
- In _Sword World RPG_ , the phenomenon of a wound becoming infected means that _the container was broken and deformed, causing the contents to become unbalanced_ . The logic goes that the greater the wound, the greater the risk of infection. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04263))_

### Chapter 12: Various Checks / Poison Treatment 12.2.1.1 and Detox Methods

- Just like in our world, in Alecrast, using poison as a weapon is an immoral act. If a player character uses poison to defeat an enemy, they are sure to be treated as a coward and looked down upon by the public. Therefore, a proud warrior does _not_ want to use poison. On the other hand, assassins, evil rune masters, dark priests, etc. _do_ like to use poison. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04268))_
- To remove a poison with magic, the caster's final score (magic power + 2D roll) must be greater than or equal to the poison's **toxicity score** . Once a detox fails, the same caster cannot attempt detox magic until their magic power increases (and even if they try, they will always fail). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04270))_
- Even if you detoxify someone _after_ they die from poison, they will _not_ come back to life. Some poisons have symptoms similar to illnesses, but they are _not_ natural illnesses and _cannot_ be cured with _Cure Disease_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04271))_
- The spirit magic _Purification_ can make liquid poison harmless if the final score when casting the magic is greater than or equal to than the toxicity score. However, it’s _not_ possible to drive off a poison that’s _already_ entered the body. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04272))_
- Therefore, a proud warrior does _not_ want to use poison. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04268))_

It has no effect on monsters that have no life such as undead, golems, etc. Some mythical and magical beasts have strong life force that makes them immune to poison. - Monsters that use poison as weapons are immune to their _own_ poisons (but are still affected by _other_ poisons). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04275))_
- It has no effect on monsters that have no life such as undead, golems, etc. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04274))_

### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 《 Usage 》

- The **usage** of the poison is written in parentheses after the name. These are divided into **injected** , **inhaled** , **dose** , and **touch** . _Injected_ poisons are poisons that need to be delivered directly into the bloodstream, usually in liquid form. It's applied to small daggers and needles and used as traps on doors and treasure chests. It can be applied to knives and arrows to increase their killing power, but the poison usually wears off after about three uses and loses its effect, so it must be reapplied frequently. Many creatures, such as giant scorpions, poisonous snakes, etc. use injected poison as a weapon. This type of poison only takes effect if you lose 1 or more points of life force when you are hurt by a poisoned blade. Even if the blade hits, if it doesn't cause a wound, it has no effect. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04282))_
- _Inhaled_ poisons are in gas or powder form and take effect when inhaled through the respiratory tract. This is also often used as a trap, but another method is to store it in a breakable bottle and use it by slamming it into your opponent. In rare cases, some creatures use poison gas as a weapon. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04283))_
- If you’re trapped in a room filled with poison gas, you must make a resistance roll every round. If you hold your breath, you will not be affected by the gas, but of course you will not be able to speak or cast spells while holding your breath. An adventurer can only hold their breath for a number of rounds equal to their life force. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04284))_
- _Dose_ poison is used by mixing it into food or drink and comes in powder or liquid form. It is mainly used for assassinations, so adventurers will barely use it. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04285))_
- _Touch_ poison comes in liquid or powder form and can penetrate through the skin. There are not many types of them, and as expected they are often used as traps. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04286))_

### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 《 Types of Poison 》

- Poisons are sometimes labeled as **magic** , **mind** , or **damage** , depending on how they affect you. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04288))_
- Those that say _magic_ are poisons that have magical effects. These are basically magical items, so they respond to the ancient magic called _Sense Magic_ . Magical poisons and their effects can be removed with _Dispel Magic_ as well as _Purification_ , _Cure Poison_ , etc. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04289))_
- Those that say _mind_ are poisons that affect the target's mind. The holy magic _Sanity_ is effective. The poison's effect also vanishes when another effect appears due to magic that belongs to _mind_ . Poisons that are both _magic_ and _mind_ are equivalent to magic that has a _target score_ . The _target score_ of a poison is the same as its _toxicity score_ (described later). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04290))_
- Those that say _damage_ are poisons that damage life force (points), but there are common rules for these types of poisons. Each poison has its own unique strike power and bonus damage. If this type of poison enters your body, you will suffer this damage. Make a strike roll as you would for any magic that deals damage. If you roll 10 or greater, it becomes a critical. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04292))_
- Those affected by the poison can reduce this damage. For characters, the score is adventurer level, and for monsters, it’s monster level. After deducting this score, if 1 or more damage remains, your life force (points) will be subtracted by the amount of damage that remains. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04293))_
- Those affected by the poison can attempt a life force resistance roll. If you successfully resist, the damage will be reduced (in this case, strike power will be -10 and no criticals will occur) or you will not suffer any damage at all. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04294))_
- After deducting this score, if 1 or more damage remains, your life force (points) will be subtracted by the amount of damage that remains. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04293))_

### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 《 Toxicity Score 》

- Each poison has a **toxicity score** . When the poison enters the body, make a life force resistance roll (for monsters, a life _point_ resistance roll) using the toxicity score as the target score. If you fail, the effect will appear, but if you succeed, the effect will only appear incomplete or won't appear at all. The toxicity scores listed here are just examples; by diluting the substance, the toxicity score can be made as low as desired, and by concentrating it, it can be made somewhat greater. When you cast the holy magic _Cure Poison_ on someone who has been poisoned, compare the final score when casting the magic to this toxicity score. If the final score is equal to or greater than the toxicity score, the poison can be removed by the magic. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04298))_
- If you fail, the effect will appear, but if you succeed, the effect will only appear incomplete or won't appear at all. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04298))_

### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 《 Rarity 》

- This represents how famous the poison is. The higher this score, the rarer the poison. An adventurer must make a success roll using _sage skill level + intelligence bonus_ as the baseline score, and if they succeed, they will know about the poison. You can also _guess_ the name of the poison when you see the symptoms of the poisoned person. If your success roll is 4 or more above the **rarity** , you can also know how to _make_ the poison and its antidote (if it has one). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04300))_
- However, even if you know how to make it, whether or not you _can_ make it is another matter. Some poisons require very rare ingredients. If a player declares that they want to make a poison (or antidote), the game master is responsible for deciding which materials are needed. The time it takes to mix a poison (or antidote) varies from one hour to one week. Also, poisons with magical effects can _only_ be mixed by sorcerers. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-04301))_
