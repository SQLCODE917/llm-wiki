## Source record

An ancient Greek hand‑powered device from ~150‑100 BC that tracks celestial bodies and eclipses.

## Key supported claims

- The mechanism is an ancient Greek hand‑powered orrery (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- By turning a crank it displays the Sun, Moon, phases, eclipses, and Olympic cycle (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Its gear precision implies a sophisticated workshop tradition (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Inscription evidence suggests it may have originated in Corinth or Syracuse (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Research used micro‑focus X‑ray tomography to read hidden inscriptions (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
