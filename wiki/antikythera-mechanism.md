## Source record

Ancient Greek orrery, oldest known analog computer.

## Key supported claims

- Mechanism is ancient Greek orrery, oldest analog computer. (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Retrieved from shipwreck off Antikythera island, dated 150–100 BC. (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Research used tomographic imaging, including microfocus X-ray CT to read hidden inscriptions. (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)
- Mechanism's sophistication not matched until medieval astronomical clocks. (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md)

## Technical details

### `technical-atom-504c4ffc755a724a` procedure

Citation: (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md normalized:L1-L26)

By turning a hand crank, the user could advance a pointer across a dial showing the position of the Sun and Moon in the zodiac, the phase of the Moon, and the dates of solar and lunar eclipses predicted by the Saros cycle.

### `technical-atom-9395fd164850f053` exception

Citation: (raw/antikythera-mechanism.md normalized:L1-L26)

Its gear-cutting precision implies an established workshop tradition, yet no comparable device or workshop has been found.
