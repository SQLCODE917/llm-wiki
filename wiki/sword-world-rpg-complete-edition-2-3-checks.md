## Source record

This chapter explains how baseline and target scores combine into a success roll check, outlines automatic success/failure, and details the mechanics of difficulty checks. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)

## Key supported claims

- Once the baseline score and target score are determined, a success roll **check** can finally be made. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)
- However, a player is given only _one_ criteria to determine whether the success roll they made for a difficulty check is a success or a failure. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)
- The game master does not say whether the result is _the action succeeded_ or _the action failed_, because a difficulty check is made when the success or failure of the action is not known to the character. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)
- So you decide to check for traps, thinking that carelessly opening this door might activate a trap. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)
- To make a difficulty check, the game master also rolls the dice (2D), and the result may be hidden from players. (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.30-33)
