# 1.1 Ability Scores

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about 1.1 ability scores:

## Statements

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores

- Just as there are many different kinds of people in the real world, strong (or weak), smart (or dumb), etc., there are many different kinds of people in Forcelia. In Sword World RPG, these basic characteristics of an adventurer are represented by numbers called **ability scores** . There are six types of ability scores: **dexterity** , **agility** , **intelligence** , **strength** , **life force** , and **mental power** . The higher the number for each, the better the ability. Ability scores apply not only to humans, but also to the various non-human races that become adventurers. Human ability scores range from 4-24 (14 on average) for each. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00159))_

- large weapons, heavy armor, etc., high strength is required. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00160))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Life Force 】

- Life force represents how well an adventurer can withstand physical injury. The higher this number, the more robust the adventurer is. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00162))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Mental Power 】

- Mental power represents the adventurer's willpower and perseverance. Using magic is hard work, and one cannot hope to cast much magic without a high mental capacity. Mental power is also related to magic resistance. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00164))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / Loss of Ability Scores and Its Impact

- Dexterity represents the dexterity of an adventurer's hands. It relates to a variety of tasks that involve the use of one's hands. This is especially important when wielding weapons in combat. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00167))_

- While adventuring, adventurers often suffer physical and mental injuries and fatigue. In _Sword World RPG_ , this is represented by a loss of life force and mental power (point reduction). If either life force or mental power fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will fall unconscious and be unable to act. The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. At this time, whether they're conscious or not depends on the situation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00168))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Agility 】

- Agility represents an adventurer's physical balance and agility. It's related to working with the whole body. In combat, it is important when evading enemy attacks. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00170))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Intelligence 】

- Intelligence represents the speed of an adventurer's mind and the amount of knowledge they hold, or in other words, their so-called smarts. It also represents the ability to perceive things, such as finding hidden objects and sensing abnormalities spur of the moment. Above all, it is important when using magic, a major attraction in fantasy. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00172))_

- If life force and mental power are reduced by wounds or exhaustion, they will recover once the wounds are treated or the exhaustion is wiped away. There are various conditions for these reductions and recoveries, which you will discover as you read through this book. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00173))_

- An ability score value is related to the success or failure of an action that an adventurer tries to perform, but in _Sword World RPG_ , success or failure is determined by a **bonus** derived from the ability score, rather than the ability score itself. The result of each ability score divided by 6 (rounded down) will be the bonus for that ability score (Table 1-1: Ability Bonuses). Each of these numbers is referred to as a _dexterity bonus_ , _intelligence bonus_ , etc., using the name of the ability score. For example, it would say something like _an adventurer with 14 dexterity has a dexterity bonus of +2_ (of course, the bonus is always 0 or a positive number, but by nature it's usually written ±0, +1, +2...... etc.) When a character's ability score increases or decreases, even temporarily, the ability bonus increases or decreases accordingly. However, temporary reductions do _not_ affect modifiers for only two scores: life force and mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00177))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills

- In addition to ability scores, _Sword World RPG_ also uses numeric values called **skills** to describe the characteristics of characters. Skills have a greater impact on a character's actions than ability scores. This chapter explains the basics of skills. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00221))_

- Experience points can also be used to increase your character's ability scores. ### Chapter 9: Notes on Success Rolls / Setting Target Scores and Difficulties

- To make a success roll, you must decide on the _skill to be used_ , the _relevant ability score_ , and the _target score or difficulty_ . Of these, skill and ability score should be clear immediately as long as you understand the _abilities_ included under each skill. The problem is the target score or difficulty. There is no problem with things that are clearly stipulated in the rules, such as climbing, hit checks in combat, etc. but things that are not will have to be set by the game master each time they are needed. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03599))_

### Chapter 9: Notes on Success Rolls / 9.4 Automatic Success Restrictions / Should the Difficulty Be Clear?

- During a difficulty check, it is up to the game master to decide whether or not to reveal the difficulty. However, the difficulty _should_ be made as clear as possible. When a player makes a success roll, they use the _baseline score (skill level + ability bonus)_ and the _final score_ attained from the dice roll to infer the success or failure of the action. At this time, if you don't know the difficulty, it’ll be difficult to guess. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03615))_

### Chapter 10: Notes on Combat / Rules for Sleeping Adventurers

- If a sleeping adventurer is approached by a monster, the baseline score is _adventurer level + intelligence bonus_ , and if you succeed on a resistance roll against target score 9, you can wake up before being attacked. If the monster has the ability to sneak up on you, the target score will be even higher. If they approach using _stealthy step_ , etc. their final score will become the target score. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03689))_

### 13.2.9 Undead / Servant

- A mummy is a monster transformed by a magical ritual in _ancient_ , and is buried together in a tomb as the keeper of its master's coffin. When a miscreant such as a graverobber appears, the mummy is given the mission to awaken and attack the intruder. If a mummy's attack hits, in addition to physical damage, a _curse_ will befall you. The character must make a mental power resistance roll against target score 13. If they fail, they will no longer be able to recover any reductions in ability scores, including life force and mental power. Let's say an adventurer under this curse is bound with a rope and the game master says, _Well, you have 0 agility and are disabled_ . At this time, even if the adventurer's rope is broken, his agility will _not_ recover from 0 and he will still be unable to take any action! They’ll no longer be able to use holy magic, but if they had a holy magic skill of 9th level or greater, they'll be able to use dark magic at the same skill level. The scores given here are for an average vampire and will vary depending on the ability scores it had previously. If you take that into account, its dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength will be _previous +6_ , and its life force and mental power will be _previous +12_ . Vampires have the ability to move while floating in the air. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-05799))_
