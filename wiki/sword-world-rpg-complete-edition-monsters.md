This page summarizes the monster types and catalog from Chapter 13 of the Sword World RPG - Complete Edition, focusing on the 12 main monster types and the structure of the monster data.

## Monster Types

There are 12 types of monsters in Alecrast: **fae**, **youma**, **spirits**, **titans**, **mythical beasts**, **magical beasts**, **animals**, **plants**, **undead**, **magical creatures**, **daemons**, **humans**, and **others**. This classification is based on the types set by Laverna, the famous witch of Orphan, who compiled the _Natural History of Alecrast_.

Each monster type has distinct characteristics and behaviors. The types include:

- **Fae**: Demi-human races from the fairy world, generally friendly towards humans but not actively seeking connection.
- **Youma**: Evil personalities among the fae, hostile towards fae and humans alike.
- **Spirits**: Energy rather than living beings, closely related to natural elements and emotions.
- **Titans**: Origin unclear, some are fae or youma, others are directly born from primordial titans.
- **Mythical Beasts and Magical Beasts**: General terms for monsters with living bodies, including ancient creatures like dragons and artificially created creatures by ancient sorcerers.
- **Animals**: Basic animals similar to those in our world, with some unusual sizes or extinct species.
- **Plants**: Plants that move and prey on animals and humans under the influence of magic.
- **Undead**: Exist through forces opposite to positive life force, despising creatures with positive life force.
- **Magical Creatures**: Pseudo-life forms created using ancient magic, given temporary bodies.
- **Daemons**: Underworld monsters with wicked personalities, connected to dark gods and dark magic.
- **Humans**: Includes villains such as bandits and dark priests.
- **Others**: Monsters not belonging to any of the 12 types, such as lycanthropes and red caps.

## Monster Catalog

The monster catalog in Chapter 13.2 provides data for various monsters, including:

- Monster Level: Corresponds to a character's adventurer level, indicating the monster's general strength.
- Rarity: Indicates how well-known the monster is.
- Agility: Represents the quickness of the monster.
- Movement Speed: Indicates the speed at which the monster moves.
- Number: The typical number of monsters encountered.
- Frequency: How often the monster is encountered.
- Intellect: The intelligence level of the monster.
- Reaction: The monster's reaction towards humans.
- Attack Points: The damage potential of the monster's attacks.
- Strike Points: The monster's ability to strike.
- Evasion Points: The monster's ability to avoid attacks.
- Defense Points: The monster's ability to defend against attacks.
- Life Points/Resistance: The monster's life points and resistance to damage.
- Mental Points/Resistance: The monster's mental points and resistance to mental effects.
- Special Abilities: Unique abilities the monster possesses.
- Habitat: The environment where the monster is typically found.
- Perception: The monster's sensory abilities.
- Languages: The languages the monster can speak.

This data is used to determine how difficult it is for a monster to die and how it interacts with players and other creatures in the game.
