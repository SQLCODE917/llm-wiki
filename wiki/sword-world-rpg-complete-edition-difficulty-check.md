A difficulty check in _Sword World RPG_ is used when the outcome of an action cannot be clearly determined. Unlike target score checks where success or failure is obvious, difficulty checks are used for actions where the character cannot tell if they succeeded or failed. 

For example, finding a trap or sneaking up on a sleeping monster are difficulty checks. When a character attempts to find a trap, they might not know if they actually found the trap or if there simply were no traps to find. Similarly, when sneaking up on a sleeping monster, the character might think they succeeded, but the monster could be pretending to sleep.

Difficulty checks are determined by the game master and are often represented as a difficulty number. The character makes a success roll, and the game master compares the result against the difficulty. If the character's final score (baseline score + 2D) meets or exceeds the difficulty, the action is considered successful. If it's less than the difficulty, the action fails.

The key distinction between difficulty checks and target score checks is that in difficulty checks, the character cannot tell whether the action succeeded or failed. This creates a different dynamic where the game master must decide whether to reveal the difficulty to the players or not.
