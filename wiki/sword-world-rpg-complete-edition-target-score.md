A target score in _Sword World RPG_ is used for success checks when the outcome of an action is clearly defined. If a character succeeds or fails in their action, it is a target score check. For example, jumping over a cliff or making a hit check in combat are target score checks.

Target scores are determined by the game master based on the difficulty of the task, often using the table in Chapter 9.1 "Setting Target Scores and Difficulties" (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.135-138). The target score is compared against the player's final score, which is the sum of their skill level and ability bonus, plus the result of the dice roll.

Target score checks are used for actions where success or failure is unambiguous. For example, if a character attempts to climb a wall, they either reach the top or fall, which is a clear outcome that can be measured against a target score.

In contrast to difficulty checks, target scores are used when the character can definitively tell whether the action succeeded or failed. For instance, if a character attempts to jump over a cliff, they succeed if their final score meets or exceeds the target score, and they fail if it does not.

The **target score** represents the difficulty of an action. It is determined by the game master based on factors such as the opponent's experience or the complexity of the task.

Target scores for certain actions can be found in _Part 2: Game Master Section_.

In success rolls, the target score is compared against the final score (baseline score + 2D) to determine if the action succeeds or fails.

- If the final score is **equal to or greater than** the target score, the action is successful.
- If the final score is **less than** the target score, the action fails.

The greater the target score, the more difficult the action. For example, hitting a seasoned warrior is more difficult than hitting an amateur with no fighting experience.
