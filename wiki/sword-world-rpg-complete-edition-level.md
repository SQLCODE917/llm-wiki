# Level

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about level:

## Statements

### 1.3.1 Skill Basics

- There are several types of skills, which are divided into several groups. For each skill, a character's proficiency is represented by a level from 0~10. 0 levels indicates that someone is completely unrelated to that skill, while 10 levels represents the highest proficiency that a normal human can attain. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-00197))_

### 1.3.3 Adventurer Level

- In Sword World RPG, a character's capability as an adventurer is determined by their adventurer skill level. The highest level among the adventurer skills that a character has is called that character's adventurer level . For example, a character with priest skill level 1, fighter skill level 4 (these are adventurer skills), sailor skill level 5, and fisher skill level 2 (these are general skills) would be at adventurer level 4 . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-00212))_

### 1.4.5 Character Growth via Starting Experience Points

- Characters can spend their starting experience points to gain skill levels. For example, if you want to acquire 1 level in ranger, you can do so by subtracting 500 from your starting experience points. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-00346))_

### 【 Attack Power 】

### 5.1.3 Learning Magic

- Magic is not omnipotent. It is only possible to achieve a defined and limited set of effects by weaving certain words (in runes), called spells . There are several types of spells, each with different effects. Depending on the strength of their effects, spells are divided into levels from 1-10 . 1st level spells are very rudimentary, while 10th level spells are very powerful. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-01092))_

- However, working with higher level (i.e., more advanced) spells requires a high rune master skill. A rune master can use all magic up to their skill level, but cannot use magic of a higher level than their current skill. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-01093))_

### Chapter 11: Notes on Magic / [ Blood Protection ]

- The caster can expand the effect to provide a greater defense effect. However, in order to do this, you must consume an extra amount of your life force as well as your mental power. ### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 《 Progression Speed/Progression Intensity/ Healing Score 》

- After a certain period of time has elapsed after becoming ill, the illness either progresses further or gets better. The degree of progression of an illness is indicated by a score called depth . Immediately after contracting an illness, the depth is 1. After the time indicated by progression speed has elapsed since you contracted the illness, roll two dice and look at the result. If the roll is less than the progression intensity , the disease gets worse and the depth increases by 1. If the roll is greater than or equal to the progression intensity but less than the healing score , the illness neither worsens nor improves, and the depth remains the same. If the result is greater than or equal to the healing score , the illness will be cured and the depth will be reduced by 1. When the depth becomes 0, you are completely recovered. The patient's adventurer level and life force bonus have no influence on this check. Adventurer level may also be hero level, but even if you're a hero, you cannot win against illness . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-04917))_
