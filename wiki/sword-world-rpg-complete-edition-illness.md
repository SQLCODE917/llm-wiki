In _Sword World RPG_, illness is defined as a state in which the balance of spirit power within the body is disrupted. The concept of bacterial or viral contagion does not exist. There are two types of symptoms that can appear. One is due to **poison** and the other is due to **illness**. In addition, a wound may become **infected**, which is treated as an illness.

Illness is a state where the spirit power within the body remains unbalanced and in a fixed state. Think of it as the spirits inside your body that are under the illusion that a false equilibrium is the correct one. Illness infection occurs when spirits transmit information about the incorrect equilibrium state to spirits in other human bodies. The unknown spirit of life heals illness by showing other spirits in the body how to achieve balance.

In _Sword World RPG_, the phenomenon of a wound becoming infected means that _the container was broken and deformed, causing the contents to become unbalanced_. The logic goes that the greater the wound, the greater the risk of infection.

## **Treatment**

Illnesses can be treated with the spirit magic _Restore Health_ or the holy magic _Cure Disease_.

## **Progression and Healing**

After a certain period of time has elapsed after becoming ill, the illness either progresses further or gets better. The degree of progression of an illness is indicated by a score called **depth**. Immediately after contracting an illness, the _depth_ is 1. After the time indicated by **progression speed** has elapsed since you contracted the illness, roll two dice and look at the result. If the roll is less than the **progression intensity**, the disease gets worse and the _depth_ increases by 1. If the roll is greater than or equal to the **progression intensity** but less than the **healing score**, the illness neither worsens nor improves, and the _depth_ remains the same. If the result is greater than or equal to the **healing score**, the illness will be cured and the _depth_ will be reduced by 1. When the _depth_ becomes 0, you are completely recovered. The patient's adventurer level and life force bonus have _no_ influence on this check. Adventurer level may also be hero level, but _even if you're a hero, you cannot win against illness_.

Even if a shaman cannot use the spell _Restore Health_, they can still work with one type of spirit to alleviate symptoms. For example, by working with salamander, fever, which is the main symptom of a cold, can be alleviated. When a shaman performs such a treatment, they gain a +1 bonus on progression/healing checks.

In addition to these, depending on the illness, there are herbs and special medicines that can provide bonuses to progression/healing checks.

When the holy magic _Cure Disease_ or _Refresh_ , or the spirit magic _Restore Health_ are cast, the caster must make a success roll using their magic power as the baseline score. The target score is double the _healing score_ (added in parentheses). If you succeed, the _depth_ of the illness is reduced by 1 and you can make an additional success roll. You can continue to make this check until you fail or until the illness is completely cured. If this does _not_ completely cure the illness, it’ll be _useless_ to cast these spells on the same patient until the caster's magic power increases.

If a healer who knows the true nature of an illness cares for the patient for more than one hour per day, they can make a similar check using that healer's _healer skill level + intelligence bonus_ as the baseline score. However, this must be at each progression speed of the illness (i.e. immediately after the illness's progression check), and you can only make one success roll. However, even if they fail, the same healer can continue to care for the patient. If after some time has passed, an illness progression check occurs, the healer can also make a healing check.

## **Fatal Depth**

If, as an illness progresses, its _depth_ reaches the **fatal depth**, the character will die from the illness.

## **Infection Power**

Indicates the infectiousness of the illness. If it's **very strong**, it has the infection power of a plague. **Strong** is about the same level as influenza. As in the example, **moderate** means it's just a cold, and **weak** means it's not very infectious. When it's **very weak**, it's not infectious and is unlikely to spread to other people. When an illness is _very strong_ or _strong_, there is a high possibility that the infection will spread to the healer, etc. who is caring for the patient. If the game master determines that there is a possibility of contracting the illness, have the character make a progression check for the illness (compare a 2D roll with progression intensity). If they fail, 164 the character has contracted the illness at _depth_ 1.
