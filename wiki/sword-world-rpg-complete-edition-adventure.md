### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores

- Just as there are many different kinds of people in the real world, strong (or weak), smart (or dumb), etc., there are many different kinds of people in Forcelia. In Sword World RPG, these basic characteristics of an adventurer are represented by numbers called **ability scores** . There are six types of ability scores: **dexterity** , **agility** , **intelligence** , **strength** , **life force** , and **mental power** . The higher the number for each, the better the ability. Ability scores apply not only to humans, but also to the various non-human races that become adventurers. Human ability scores range from 4-24 (14 on average) for each. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00159))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Life Force 】

- Life force represents how well an adventurer can withstand physical injury. The higher this number, the more robust the adventurer is. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00162))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / Loss of Ability Scores and Its Impact

- While adventuring, adventurers often suffer physical and mental injuries and fatigue. In _Sword World RPG_ , this is represented by a loss of life force and mental power (point reduction). If either life force or mental power fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will fall unconscious and be unable to act. The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. At this time, whether they're conscious or not depends on the situation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00168))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / Skill Types

- First, skills are divided into two types: **adventurer skills** and **general skills** . Adventurer skills, as the name implies, are acquired by player characters, the adventurers, or by equivalent characters. There are eight of these: **fighter** , **thief** , **priest** , **sorcerer** , **shaman** , **sage** , **ranger** , and **bard** . There are also two adventurer skills that _cannot_ be acquired by a player character: **dark priest** and **dragon priest** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00232))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / Adventurer Level

- In Sword World RPG, a character's capability as an adventurer is determined by their adventurer skill level. The highest level among the adventurer skills that a character has is called that character's **adventurer level** . For example, a character with priest skill level 1, fighter skill level 4 (these are adventurer skills), sailor skill level 5, and fisher skill level 2 (these are general skills) would be at adventurer level **4** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00241))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / 1.3.4 Explanation of Adventurer Skills

- Adventurer skills are the most important thing for an adventurer in _Sword World RPG._ This is because which adventurer skills a character has acquired will determine what that character can do, and will reveal that character's characteristics. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00244))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / 【 Rune Master 】

- The five adventurer skills of sorcerer, shaman, priest, dark priest, and dragon priest are sometimes collectively referred to as **rune master skills** . This is because these five classes use **runes** and have the ability to handle magic. ### Chapter 12: Various Checks / 12.2 Rules for Poison, Illness and Infection / Intensity/ Healing Score 》

- After a certain period of time has elapsed after becoming ill, the illness either progresses further or gets better. The degree of progression of an illness is indicated by a score called **depth** . Immediately after contracting an illness, the _depth_ is 1. After the time indicated by **progression speed** has elapsed since you contracted the illness, roll two dice and look at the result. If the roll is less than the **progression intensity** , the disease gets worse and the _depth_ increases by 1. If the result is greater than or equal to the _healing score_ , the illness will be cured and the _depth_ will be reduced by 1. When the _depth_ becomes 0, you are completely recovered. The patient's adventurer level and life force bonus have _no_ influence on this check.
