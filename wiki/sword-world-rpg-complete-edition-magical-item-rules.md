Magical items in the Sword World RPG are objects imbued with magical properties that can provide advantages to adventurers. These items include weapons, armor, and other artifacts.

## Types of Magical Items

Magical items are categorized into several types:

- **Magical Weapons and Armor**: These items have lasting magical properties and are often found in ancient kingdoms. The lowest value is 10 silver coins, and the highest quality are worth around 10,000 silver coins.
- **Artwork**: These are works of art created during the ancient kingdom period with great antique value.
- **Gems and Precious Metals**: Items made from gems and precious metals that can be judged as treasure or junk by using the thief skill and sage skill's _treasure appraisal_ ability.

## Rarity and Appraisal

The **rarity** of a magical item is the target score for a success roll when using the sage skill's _treasure appraisal_ ability. If the success roll results in a final score greater than or equal to this rarity, you will discover the true identity of the treasure. Some treasures may have more than one listed rarity, which can result in an incomplete appraisal.

Magical treasures can be understood through spells such as _Sense Magic_ and their specific power can be appraised using _Analyze Enchantment_.

## Enchanters

Magical items are always endowed with magical powers. The enchanter's name is written in the item's description. Some items are created by known enchanters, while others are attributed to unknown or legendary figures.

## Base Trade Price

The **base trade price** is the price at which the item is traded. If it's for sale, adventurers can buy it at the price shown here. If an adventurer sells it, the standard price is half this amount.

## Special Considerations

- Items with commands in high ancient can only be handled by those who have the sorcerer skill.
- Even items without magical power, such as books and scrolls, may contain valuable information about the culture of the ancient kingdom.
- The final value of the treasure is determined entirely by the player character, although it might not necessarily match the NPC's valuation.
- Adventurers should not be able to get their hands on items that are too powerful.
