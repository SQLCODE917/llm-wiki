# Score

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about score:

## Statements

- The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00173))_
- The result of each ability score divided by 6 (rounded down) will be the bonus for that ability score (Table 1-1: Ability Bonuses). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00183))_
- A character's six ability scores are each the totals of two of these eight subability scores. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00328))_
- Once ability scores are determined, sub-ability scores are _never_ used during the game. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00339))_
- An ability bonus equals the ability score divided by 6 (rounded down), as shown under 1.1: Ability Scores. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00342))_
- Once sub-ability scores are determined, ability scores are calculated by addition. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00515))_
- His baseline score for attacks is _4_ and the target score for hitting a goblin is _10_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00577))_
- This final score should be _equal to or greater than_ the target score. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00578))_
- The _toxicity score_ of ghoul poison is 10, which is the target score. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00674))_
- The baseline score is _life force resistance (adventurer level + life force bonus)_ and the target score is _7_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01089))_
- The baseline score is _4_ (magic power) and the target score is _9_ (goblin's mental point resistance). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01259))_
- The target score is a score called the monster’s **magic intensity** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01271))_
- If this final score is greater than or equal to the _target score_ originally set for that first magic that was cast, then that first magic can be nullified. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01429))_
- In this case, according to the definition of _magic with a target score_ , the magic cast after will _not_ take effect unless its final score is greater than or equal to the determined _target score_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01442))_

## Technical atoms

> Context: adventurer tries to perform, but in _Sword World RPG_ , success or failure is determined by a **bonus** derived from the ability score, rather than the ability score itself. The result of each ability score divided by 6 (rounded down) will be the bonus for that ability score (Table 1-1: Ability Bonuses). Each of these numbers is referred to as a _dexterity bonus_ , _intelligence bonus_ , etc., using the name of the ability score. For example, it would say something like _an adventurer with 14 dexterity has a dexterity bonus of +2_ (of course, the bonus is always 0 or a positive number, but by nature it's usually written ±0, +1, +2...... etc.) When a character's ability score increases or decreases, even temporarily, the ability bonus increases or decreases accordingly. However, temporary reductions do _not_ affect modifiers for only two scores: life force and mental power.
_(context: Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00183))_
