## Source record

Extended rules for weapons and armor that are too heavy, including penalties for using items exceeding a character's strength.

## Key supported claims

- Characters cannot hold separate weapons in each hand in basic rules, but dual wielding is possible with extended rules (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268-268).
- Characters are generally righthanded or left-handed, but some are ambidextrous (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268-268).
- If a character has weapons in both hands, there are three options for attacking with penalties (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268-268).
- This section presents extended rules for holding weapons in both hands (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268-268).
- Characters can use weapons and armor whose required strength exceeds their own strength, suffering penalties (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268-268).

### `technical-atom-2db8ec4120e9aef1` exception

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268)

Also, the required strength for _one_ of the weapons is limited to _half your own strength (rounded up)_ .

### `technical-atom-804a72b3bc7ddc15` exception

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268)

If you’re fighting with the thief skill, the required strength for _both_ weapons is only _half_ the character's strength.

### `technical-atom-aa4407a71cb121a0` exception

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.268)

If you attack with only _one_ hand, there is _no_ penalty, and if you attack with _both_ hands, the penalty is only _-2_ for each.
