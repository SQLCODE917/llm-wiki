This chapter covers magic expansion in the Sword World RPG, detailing how rune masters can increase the effectiveness and certainty of magic by consuming extra mental power.

## Magic Expansion

Magic expansion is done by doubling or tripling the amount of mental power consumed. At this time, you do not double or triple the base mental power cost. Instead, you divide the mental power by your skill level, rounded up, or in other words, the mental power you would consume when not expanding is doubled, tripled, etc.

## Example:

Suppose a sorcerer with sorcerer skill level 2 doubles the range of his _Energy Bolt_ spell and casts it on two enemies. The base mental power cost of _Energy Bolt_ is _5_. For this reason, the mental power consumed without expansion is 5 ÷ 2, rounded up to _3_. Therefore, for a doubled expansion, the amount of mental power consumed would be 3 x 2 = **6**. You would not calculate (5 x 2) ÷ 2 = 5.

## Expansion Types

Magic expansion includes the following types:

### Targets Expansion

If you expand a spell that affects one enemy (or ally), it can instead affect multiple at the same time. If it's two or three targets, the mental power consumed will also be doubled or tripled. In this case, you must roll the dice separately for each target to determine if they resist the magic, and other effects such as damage must also be determined separately. For spells that can perform this expansion, **targets** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

### Area Expansion

If you expand a spell that affects a specific area, it can affect a larger area than originally intended. If the area is doubled or tripled, the mental power consumed will also be doubled or tripled. You can also expand an area by a fraction (x1.5, x8/3, etc.), in which case the fraction is rounded up when determining the multiplier for mental power consumed. So for expansions such as x 1.5 and x 8/3, the cost would be double and triple, respectively. Since it's the area that gets expanded, the radius of a circle, a side of a square, etc., can only be increased by the square root of the expansion rate. Please see Table 5-1: Area and Size Expansion. For spells that can perform this expansion, **area** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

### Volume Expansion

Some spells that affect a space or object can have their volume expanded. If the volume is expanded by two or three times, the mental power consumed will also be doubled or tripled. Rounding should be handled the same way you would for area expansion. Since it's the volume that gets expanded, the radius of a sphere, a side of a cube, etc., can only be increased by the cube root of the expansion rate. Please see Table 5-1: Area and Size Expansion. For spells that can perform this expansion, **target volume** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

**Table 5-1: Area and Size Expansion**

|Expansion Rate|Area Expansion Length|Volume Expansion Length|
|---|---|---|
|x 2|1.4|1.3|
|x 3|1.7|1.4|
|x 4|2|1.6|
|x 5|2.2|1.7|
|x 6|2.4|1.8|
|x 7|2.6|1.9|
|x 8|2.8|2.0|
|x 9|3.0|2.1|
|x 10|3.2|2.2|

Note: On the table, _expansion rate_ is the multiplier for mental power consumed. When you cast a spell by consuming that multiplied amount of mental power, multiply the number in _area expansion length_ or _volume expansion length_ by the number (radius, side) that represents the spell's _area of effect_, and the result will be the spell's _area_. For spells cast on a flat surface, use the number in the _area expansion length_ column, and for spells cast into space, use the number in the _volume expansion length_ column. For example, when you cast the ancient magic _Light_, whose area is _A space with a 10 meter radius_, by consuming _double_ the mental power, the resulting area will be a spherical space with a radius of _10 x 1.3 = **13 meters**_.

### Duration Expansion

For spells whose effects last a certain amount of time, you can expand that duration. If the duration is doubled or tripled, the mental power consumed will also be doubled or tripled. You can also increase it by a fraction, such as x1.5, in which case the fraction is rounded up when determining the multiplier for mental power consumed. For spells that can perform this expansion, **duration** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

### Distance Expansion

Magic basically only works up to a certain distance. However, there are spells that can extend their reach. If reach distance is doubled or tripled, the mental power consumed will also be doubled or tripled. You can also increase it by a fraction, such as x1.5, in which case the fraction is rounded up when determining the multiplier for mental power consumed. For spells that can perform this expansion, **distance** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

### Damage Expansion

For spells that deal damage, expansion can increase the certainty of that damage. If you double or triple the amount of mental power you consume, you can make a damage check (strike roll) twice or three times. The damage you will deal is equal to the greatest of all the strike rolls you made in this way (not the total). For spells that can perform this expansion, **damage certainty** is written in the expansion section of the magic details.

### Final Score Increase

There's some magic whose effects are negated or reduced if they're resisted by the target, but will also let you increase the magic's final score (resist target score) in order to prevent this. This increase in final score may also take effect if a nullifying spell is cast later. If you double or triple the amount of mental power you consume, you can add a +1 or +2 bonus to your final score. For spells that can perform this expansion, **final score** is written in the expansion section of the magic details. This expansion requires a deep proficiency in magic. Therefore, it cannot be done infinitely. Final score can only be increased up to a maximum of _caster's rune master skill level ÷ 2 (rounded up)_. That's up to _+1 (double)_ for 1st~2nd level, _+2 (triple)_ for 3rd~4th level, and _+5 (sextuple)_ for 9th~10th level.

### Effect Expansion

Some expansions don't fall into any of the above types. For spells that can perform these special expansions, **effect** is written in the expansion section of the magic details, and an explanation is given under each spell.

## Combined Expansion

Some magic allows for multiple expansion types. In these cases, you can perform multiple expansions at the same time. However, at this time, the amount of mental power consumed will become extremely large. By multiplying the rates determined for each expansion, you can determine the total multiplied rate of mental power consumed.

### Example:

Suppose a sorcerer casts an _Energy Bolt_ on three enemies (x 3 expansion) with a +1 to his final score (x 2 expansion) and he makes 4 damage checks for each (x 4 expansion). In this case, the multiplied rate of mental power consumed is 3 x 2 x 4 = **24!**

## Magic Contraction

Magic can also be contracted as opposed to expanded. However, this does not reduce the amount of mental power consumed.

## Magic Types

There's several kinds of magic that share the same characteristics. These characteristics are presented here as magic types:

### Damage

A spell that deals damage to the target's life force or mental power. Spells of the damage type always have a clearly defined strike power. If you manage to break through the target's resist, use the spell's strike power to determine damage by making a strike roll using the Rating Table, same as if you had hit with a weapon in weapon combat. The key number is, of course, the indicated strike power. If you roll 10 or greater during the strike roll, a critical will occur and deal even more damage, same as in weapon combat. Conversely, If you roll double ones, it does not deal any damage at all. Most magic of the damage type can still deal damage even if it's resisted by the target, only with a reduced effect. In this case, you must make the strike roll after subtracting 10 from strike power. Also, at this time, no matter how high you roll during your strike roll, a critical will not occur. In either case, the caster's magic power can be added as bonus damage to the result of the strike roll. Also, the target's adventurer level (or the monster's monster level) is subtracted from the damage. However, there's an exception if you roll double ones during your strike roll. In this case, no matter how much magic power your character (monster) has, they cannot deal damage to the target at all. Also, even magic of the damage type may in rare cases take no effect if resisted. At this time, if the target successfully resists, you cannot make a strike roll.

### Target Score

Some magic can nullify the effects of other magic. For example, the ancient magic _Dispel Magic_ nullifies the effects of most magic. Most magic is automatically nullified if a nullifying spell is used, but there are some that are not. This is magic that has a target score. When casting any magic, no matter what kind of magic it is, you must roll 2D to check whether an automatic failure (double ones) will occur or not. If you're trying to nullify magic that has a target score with another spell, you must use that other spell's 2D roll to determine your final score. This final score equals magic power + 2D roll (similar to a final score for other success rolls). If this final score is greater than or equal to the target score originally set for that first magic that was cast, then that first magic can be nullified. This target score equals the final score of the first magic that was cast (in other words, the magic you're about to nullify). Therefore, when an adventurer uses magic that has a target score, that player should note down that score and remember it. However, if you feel this is cumbersome, or if you forget to make a note, then when it becomes necessary (nullifying magic has been used), you can roll 2D on the spot to determine a final score = target score. When you use magic that nullifies other magic, if your roll is double sixes, the magic will automatically be nullified, no matter what the target score is. Conversely, if the dice roll double sixes when determining the target score (the success score of the first magic), you must roll another double sixes in order to nullify that magic.

### Counters (Negates)

When magic is cast against other magic, their effects may completely cancel each other out. For example, there are two kinds of ancient magic, _Decrease Weight_ (which makes an object lighter) and _Increase Weight_ (which makes an object heavier), but when both of these are cast on the same object at the same time, they will cancel each other out and disappear without taking effect. Such magic, that is, magic that negates the effect of other magic, but at the same time is negated by that other magic without taking its own effect, is called **counters (negates):** type. When casting counters (negates): magic to cancel the effect of certain magic, if the magic that was cast prior has a target score, then according to the definition of magic with a target score, the magic that's cast after cannot cancel the prior magic unless its final score is greater than or equal to the determined target score.

### Counters (Overpowers)

When certain magic is cast against other magic, it can cancel the effect of the prior magic but still take its own effect afterwards. This is magic of the **counters (overpowers):** type. For example, the ancient magic _Darkness_ can nullify the effects of the spirit magic _Will-O-Wisp_ and still close the surrounding area in darkness, which means _Darkness_ itself is more effective. However, this is often not automatic. One, is when the target resists. In the case of magic that does not take effect if resisted, if the target does resist it, the magic will not take its effect, and the effect of the prior magic will continue. The other, is when the prior magic has a target score. In this case, according to the definition of magic with a target score, the magic cast after will not take effect unless its final score is greater than or equal to the determined target score. If the magic cast prior has a target score and the magic cast after is resistible, please follow the procedure below to make a check.

① First, confirm the target score (= final score) of the magic cast prior (if you forgot, determine it now).

② Next, the one casting the magic must roll 2D to find their final score (by adding their magic power).

③ Compare target score in ① with final score in ②. If the final score does not reach the target score, the magic will not be cast. This ends here.

④ If final score in ② is greater than or equal to target score in ①, the target may attempt to resist. The resist target score equals the final score in ②.

⑤ If the target successfully resisted in ④, the new magic cast will not take effect. The effect of the prior magic will continue. If the target failed to resist, the new magic cast will take effect and the effect of the prior magic will be negated.

Counters (overpowers): magic does not mean that certain magic is definitely stronger than other magic. It's possible for magic A to counter (overpower) magic B, and at the same time for magic B to counter (overpower) magic A. Also, all magic counters (overpowers) itself. Therefore, if certain magic takes effect, and you cast that same magic on the same target multiple times, the effect will not be cumulative.

### Mind

This is magic that affects the target's mental state. This kind of magic can only take effect on one target at a time. If magic of the mind type is cast on a target, and other magic also of the mind type is cast on that target, the new magic will take effect, and the prior magic will be nullified. In other words, all mind magic counters (overpowers) other mind magic. Note that many are resistible magic, and some have target scores.

### Physical Ability

This is magic that affects the target's physical abilities (strength, etc.). Magic of this type can only take effect on humans, fae, and youma. Also, with magic of the physical ability type, two or more cannot affect the same target at the same time. Please resolve them as counters (overpowers) magic. It's possible for both mind and physical ability magic to each affect the same target separately.

### Weapon Enhancement

Magic that targets a weapon and enhances it. Two or more of this kind of magic cannot affect the same weapon at the same time. Please resolve them as counters (overpowers) magic. Weapons that have been given power by ancient magic, silver weapons, or mithril silver weapons are assumed to already have non-removable (see below) weapon enhancement magic cast on them. Therefore, that power cannot be nullified, so even if you cast new weapon enhancement magic, it'll be useless.

### Non-removable

Some magic, such as _Dispel Magic_ (ancient magic) and _Remove Curse_ (holy magic), can be used as counter magic that removes other magic. However, magic of the non-removable type cannot be removed by other magic. Although, the ancient magic _Perfect Cancellation_ is able to remove non-removable magic. Also, sometimes there is counter magic that counters certain magic, which if specified, will be removed by that counter magic. It's also possible to remove non-removable magic by directly using the power of a god (wicked god) with the holy/dark magic _Call God_.

### Curse

_Dispel Magic_ (ancient magic) can be used to remove most magic, but for magic of the curse type, this is not possible. However, _Remove Curse_ (holy magic) can be used to remove it.

### Detection

A spell that detects objects, people, magic power, etc. These can be targeted by the ancient magic _Counter Sense_. These spells also ignore the protection of the ancient magic _Magic Protection_.

### Focus

Magic has a fixed duration. Magic of the focus type is that in which the magic's effect lasts as long as the caster (the one who casts the magic) is focused. While focusing, the caster cannot take the following actions:
- Use other magic
- Attack another with a weapon (or barehanded)
- Move more than 3 meters in one round
- Speak words
- Any other voluntary action

Even while focusing, if another attacks you with a weapon, etc. you can still attempt to evade it. If magic is cast on you, it's also possible to resist it. Also, when the target is no longer within the magic's area of effect (out of reach, out of sight, etc.), the magic will lose its effect, even if you continue to focus. If the caster suffers damage to their life force or mental power, their focus will be interrupted and the magic's effect will be lost. If the caster wishes to voluntarily release their focus (in order to take another action), they must declare it at the beginning of the round, when no one else has made their action announcements yet. If your focus is interrupted due to damage, etc. your focus will be interrupted at the timing in which the damage is dealt.
