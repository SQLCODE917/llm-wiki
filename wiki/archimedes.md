Archimedes was an ancient Greek mathematician and engineer, renowned for his contributions to mathematics, physics, and engineering. He is credited with inventions such as the Archimedean screw and principles of buoyancy. The Antikythera mechanism may be linked to the school of Archimedes in Syracuse, suggesting a possible connection between his work and this sophisticated device.
