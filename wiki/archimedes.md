Archimedes of Syracuse (c. 287 – c. 212 BC) was a Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, inventor, and astronomer. He is considered one of the greatest mathematicians of all time and is known for his work in geometry, statics, and hydrostatics.

Archimedes made significant contributions to the understanding of the lever and the principle of buoyancy, famously exclaiming "Eureka!" upon discovering the principle of displacement. He also designed innovative machines, including the Archimedes screw for raising water and various war machines used during the Siege of Syracuse.

He is linked to the Antikythera mechanism, possibly through the school of thought in Syracuse where the device may have originated. [[antikythera-mechanism]]
