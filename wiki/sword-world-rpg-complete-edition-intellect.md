Intellect in the Sword World RPG - Complete Edition indicates the monster's degree of intellect. There are eight types: **none**, **almost none**, **obeys commands**, **animal**, **low**, **human**, **high**, and **very high**.

The intellect level of a monster affects its behavior, ability to use tools, communication, and resistance to mental effects. Different intellect levels correspond to different intelligence scores and abilities, ranging from reflexive actions with no intelligence (none) to highly intelligent beings with genius-level intellect (very high).

- **None**: The monster does not think or have instincts. It acts only on reflex. Spells that affect the mind have no effect on these monsters.
- **Almost none**: The monster has almost no intellect. They have no advanced thinking and have almost no means of communicating their intentions. They only act on instinct.
- **Obeys commands**: Common among magical creatures created by magic, the undead, etc. They will faithfully carry out commands given by their creators or those who used magic to give the command, but they cannot make their own decisions. Spells that affect the minds are ineffective.
- **Animal**: The monster has the same degree of intelligence as an animal. They can distinguish their master and will follow commands (if trained). They have enough judgment ability to quickly run away if their life is in danger.
- **Low**: The monster has a low intellect. They can use primitive tools, words, and a very limited number of written characters. They're able to make accurate judgments in normal activities but are easily fooled by simple words and tricks.
- **Human**: The monster has an intellect that's almost the same as that of a human. They can handle tools, words, text, etc. freely.
- **High**: The monster has a greater intellect than the average human. For a human, you could even use the adjective _wise_.
- **Very high**: The monster's intellect is beyond the reach of normal humans. A human with this much intellect would probably go down in history as a great genius.

The intellect level also determines how the monster interacts with players and other creatures in the game, affecting combat strategies, communication, and magical resistance.
