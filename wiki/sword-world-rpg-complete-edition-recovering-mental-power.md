# 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about 5.1.6 recovering mental power:

## Statements

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores

- Just as there are many different kinds of people in the real world, strong (or weak), smart (or dumb), etc., there are many different kinds of people in Forcelia. In Sword World RPG, these basic characteristics of an adventurer are represented by numbers called **ability scores** . There are six types of ability scores: **dexterity** , **agility** , **intelligence** , **strength** , **life force** , and **mental power** . The higher the number for each, the better the ability. Ability scores apply not only to humans, but also to the various non-human races that become adventurers. Human ability scores range from 4-24 (14 on average) for each. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00159))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Mental Power 】

- Mental power represents the adventurer's willpower and perseverance. Using magic is hard work, and one cannot hope to cast much magic without a high mental capacity. Mental power is also related to magic resistance. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00164))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / Loss of Ability Scores and Its Impact

- While adventuring, adventurers often suffer physical and mental injuries and fatigue. In _Sword World RPG_ , this is represented by a loss of life force and mental power (point reduction). If either life force or mental power fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will fall unconscious and be unable to act. The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. At this time, whether they're conscious or not depends on the situation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00168))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Intelligence 】

- If life force and mental power are reduced by wounds or exhaustion, they will recover once the wounds are treated or the exhaustion is wiped away. There are various conditions for these reductions and recoveries, which you will discover as you read through this book. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00173))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Ability Bonuses 】

- An ability score value is related to the success or failure of an action that an adventurer tries to perform, but in _Sword World RPG_ , success or failure is determined by a **bonus** derived from the ability score, rather than the ability score itself. The result of each ability score divided by 6 (rounded down) will be the bonus for that ability score (Table 1-1: Ability Bonuses). Each of these numbers is referred to as a _dexterity bonus_ , _intelligence bonus_ , etc., using the name of the ability score. For example, it would say something like _an adventurer with 14 dexterity has a dexterity bonus of +2_ (of course, the bonus is always 0 or a positive number, but by nature it's usually written ±0, +1, +2...... etc.) When a character's ability score increases or decreases, even temporarily, the ability bonus increases or decreases accordingly. However, temporary reductions do _not_ affect modifiers for only two scores: life force and mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00177))_

### Chapter 2: Success Rolls / Resistance Rolls

- A success roll made using _adventurer level + life force bonus_ as the baseline score is specifically referred to as a **life force resistance roll** . Similarly, a success roll made using _adventurer level + mental power bonus_ as the baseline score is specifically called a **mental power resistance roll** , and the two are collectively called simply **resistance rolls** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00645))_

- Therefore, these baseline scores are also defined by the terms **life force resistance** and **mental power resistance** , among others. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00647))_

- Life force resistance rolls are made to avoid or reduce the effects of physical dangers such as poison, while mental power resistance rolls are made to avoid or reduce the effects of magic or curses. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00648))_

- In particular, all checks against magic, even if it causes physical damage, are made with mental power resistance rolls. Checks against special attacks by monsters that are magical in nature are also made with mental power resistance rolls. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00649))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / Example:

- The evil sorcerer Gorman (magic power 5) unleashes _Lightning_ on the three adventurers. First, Gorman rolls 2D and adds his own magic power. If we assume his 2D roll is _8_ , each of the three adventurers must make a mental power resistance roll, with a target score of 5 (magic power) + 8 (roll) = _13_ . Suppose that the baseline score of all three of their mental power resistance rolls is _4_ . If their rolls are each _2_ (double ones!), _6_ , and _9_ , then only the _last_ one is able to resist the _Lightning_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01262))_

- Handling double ones and double sixes is no different from when there’s only one target. If the side casting the magic rolls double ones, the magic has _no_ effect on _any_ targets. If the side casting the magic rolls double sixes, _no_ _one_ can resist, except for those who _also_ roll double sixes. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01263))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Consuming Mental Power

- Adventurers cannot use magic endlessly. When using magic, an adventurer must temporarily _reduce_ their mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01272))_

- Each magic has a score called **base mental power cost** . This base mental power cost, divided by the corresponding rune master skill level (rounded up), equals the actual mental power consumed by the character. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01273))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Example:

- A character with shaman skill level 2 uses the _Fire Bolt_ spell. The base mental power cost for _Fire Bolt_ is _5_ . The character must consume 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5, rounding up to _3_ points of mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01275))_

- A character can reduce their mental power to 0, but they _cannot_ make it negative. For example, a character with 5 points of mental power remaining _can_ use magic that costs 5 points of mental power, but _cannot_ use magic that costs _6 or more_ points of mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01276))_

- If a character’s mental power reaches _0_ , they will _lose consciousness_ . The character cannot regain consciousness unless they recover their mental power to _1 point or more_ in some way. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01277))_
