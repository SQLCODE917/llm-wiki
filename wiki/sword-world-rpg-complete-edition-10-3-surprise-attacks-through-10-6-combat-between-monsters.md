## Source record

Chapter 10.3-10.6 of Sword World RPG - Complete Edition covers surprise attacks, combat in special situations, and combat between monsters.

## Key supported claims

- Game master manages NPC and monster actions in combat (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-138).
- Combat in special situations applies penalties or bonuses to attack power or evasion speed (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.139-139).
- Surprise attacks depend on game master's judgment and use a check table (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.140-140).

## Technical details

### `technical-atom-5c104310ad14d2f8` requirement

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

The game master must manage the actions of NPCs and monsters.

### `technical-atom-de28be23e3612643` requirement

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

In combat, the planned actions of NPCs and monsters must be announced at the beginning of the round.

### `technical-atom-7e5522c19ed00954` requirement

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

However, the game master should decide in advance which actions they are going to take.

### `technical-atom-87df092ac755c2de` requirement

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

They must _not_ change things based on the circumstances, such as attacking with fangs if the character used the spell _Counter Magic_ to increase their resistance, or breathing fire if the character used the spell _Quickness_ to raise their agility (evasion speed).

### `technical-atom-b9a1d616ba49ef2e` exception

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

At this time, the game master does not need to make a specific announcement.

### `technical-atom-9f5b84fc4e8bc702` worked-example

Citation: (raw/Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf p.138-140)

For example, it’s correct to announce the planned action of a hellhound with the ability to breathe fire by saying, _I'm about to do something with my mouth wide open…_ The characters have no way of knowing if the hellhound is about to bite them or breathe fire.
