The craftsman skill is a general skill in _Sword World RPG_ related to the creation of items. In _Sword World_ , all production is done by hand. Therefore, the quality of the item depends entirely on the skill of the craftsman. Products made by craftsmen are picked up by merchants and displayed in their shops. Of course, items made by _skilled_ craftsmen will be _more_ expensive, and items that are _not_ made by skilled craftsmen will be _cheaper_ . Naturally, there are many different types of craftsmen. There are craftsmen who make furniture, and there are also blacksmiths who make weapons. These techniques are secretly taught from master to apprentice, and care is taken to ensure that no secret techniques are leaked. Also, a craftsman must belong to a guild. It is illegal for anyone outside a guild to make products handled by that guild.

Owners of the craftsman skill have the following abilities:

① **Craft** [Dexterity]
② **Value Check** [Intelligence]

## **① Craft [Dexterity]**
This is a technique for creating items. If a craftsman devotes the appropriate amount of time to production (which varies depending on the price and quality of the item they're making), they will be able to create a product that complies with their level. Of course, depending on the item, you may need tools and equipment. The finished product will be picked up by a merchant at half the price listed on the price list. Items made by high level craftsmen are naturally of high quality. Of course, prices will _also_ go up. It’s up to the game master to decide what bonuses to give to these high-quality, expensive items. Success rolls are _not_ required to create items of _normal_ quality, but success rolls _are_ required for weapon and armor craftsmen to create _high_ quality weapons and armor. The target score is _15 + the decrease in required strength_ . However, you cannot create weapons or armor with a required strength of 6 or lower.

## **② Value Check [Intelligence]**
Craftsmen know how high the quality of the products they have made are, so naturally they can determine the value of items made by others. Treat this the same way as a merchant's _value check_ .
