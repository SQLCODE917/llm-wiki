## Statements

- When a monster casts magic, the procedure is different from when an adventurer casts magic. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01215))_
- In the case of _resistible_ magic, the issue is resolved from the perspective of whether the adventurer is able to resist the magic cast by the monster. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01216))_
- There is no need to roll the dice, as the magic will _definitely_ take its effect. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01216))_
- This is determined by a success roll using the character's _mental power resistance_ , that is, a _mental power resistance roll_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01216))_
- First, in the case of _non-resistible_ magic, a monster can _never_ fail. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01216))_
- The target score is a score called the monster’s **magic intensity** . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01216))_
- If the adventurer's final score is _less_ than the monster's magic intensity, they will receive the _full_ effect of the magic. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01217))_
- Also, since this is considered a success roll, it’ll be an _automatic failure_ if the 2D roll is _double ones_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01218))_
- Conversely, _double sixes_ is an _automatic success_ , so in this case, regardless of the adventurer's mental power resistance or the monster's magic intensity, that’s a _successful resist_ * and the magic will only take an incomplete effect. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01219))_
