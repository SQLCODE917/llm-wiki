# Accomplishing Missions

From [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]].

## Statements

- Most experience points are earned by accomplishing missions. When creating an adventure scenario, the game master sets one goal. If you're going into ruins to search for treasure, the goal is probably the treasure hidden there, and if you're rescuing a captured dignitary, it' s to bring that person back safely. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08820))_
- When you achieve a goal set by the game master, you will receive experience points for accomplishing missions . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08821))_
- This may be different from what it appears to be upon entering the scenario. Upon closer examination, a request to transport an item may turn out to be stolen goods, and in a change of situation, must be returned to its rightful owner. At this time, the mission will only be accomplished if it's returned to its rightful owner. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08822))_
- If you were to become one of the thieves' transport partners without realizing they're stolen goods, your mission would not be accomplished. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08823))_
- If each character achieves the goal (mission) set by the game master, they will each receive 1000 experience points. If they cannot achieve it, they will only get 500 points. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08824))_
- At this time, the mission will only be accomplished if it's returned to its rightful owner. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-de1806a5-08822))_
