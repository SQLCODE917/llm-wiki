## **14.2 Adventure Treasure and Reward Amounts**

What kind of rewards should be given for an adventure is ultimately up to the game master. However, the rewards obtained from an adventure should comply with the level of risk. If you could obtain a large amount of treasure through an easy adventure, that would take the fun out of the game. The opposite can also result in players losing their motivation.

To determine the value (upper limit) of the treasure that can be obtained in terms of silver coins, multiply the adventurer levels of all the player characters, then multiply the total by 500.

Of course, depending on the contents of the scenario and the actions of the player characters, it's fine to introduce unexpected treasure, or to even have an adventure that results in a deficit. Game masters should be aware of how much property and equipment the player characters have.
