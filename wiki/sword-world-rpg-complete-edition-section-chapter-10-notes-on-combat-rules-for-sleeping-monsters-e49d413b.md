## Statements

- To get within 5 meters of a sleeping monster, you must successfully perform the thief skill _stealthy step_ . If you succeed on a success roll using _thief skill level + dexterity bonus_ as the baseline score, the monster will not wake up. If you fail, the monster will sense your presence and wake up immediately. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03678))_
- Attacks against sleeping monsters are _always_ a success unless you roll 2D and get _double ones_ on your hit check (even when attacking a sleeping opponent, double ones is always a failure). Furthermore, when making a damage check, you can reduce the weapon's critical target by -1. If you are attacking with the _thief_ skill, your critical target will be -2. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03679))_
- If you attack a sleeping monster but do not deal any damage, or if you deal damage but are unable to reduce its life points to 0, the monster will wake up immediately. It will probably become angry and counterattack the adventurer starting on the next round. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03681))_
- If there is combat, etc. going on in the surrounding area or a loud noise is made, the monster will wake up immediately. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03682))_

- If the opponent you're fighting is a creature that lives on land, such as a human, they will suffer the same penalty. However, creatures that live underwater, such as mermen, do not suffer any penalties other than weapon restrictions. Conversely, these creatures become slower on land and typically suffer a -4 penalty to hit and evasion. Some creatures, such as hydra and scylla, can act both in water and on land without penalty. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03685))_

If it's natural sleep, there is no need to roll and it'll always wake up. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-03686))_
