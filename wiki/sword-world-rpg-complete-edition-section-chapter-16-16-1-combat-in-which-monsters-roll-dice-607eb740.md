## Statements

- In Sword World RPG , the monster side (game master) rarely rolls dice during combat. This reduces the burden on the game master and allows him or her to focus on other parts. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11166))_
- However, some game masters may also want to roll the dice. By rolling the dice yourself, you can feel like you're playing the game. When both sides roll the dice, the range of numbers will expand and the enjoyment can increase. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11167))_
- In an emergency, the game master can control the results of his or her own dice, making it easier to weave a story. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11168))_
- This is because these advantages exist. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11169))_
- Here we'll introduce the procedure by which the game master also rolls the dice and performs combat. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11170))_
- This is because these advantages exist. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11169))_

## Statements by subsection

### Chapter 16: / 16.1 Combat in Which Monsters Roll Dice / Attacks From Monsters Against Characters

- A score equal to a monster's attack points - 7 is written next to it in parenthesis. This is used here. The game master should roll 2D and add it to the number in parenthesis. This is the target score for a character to evade the monster's attack. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11172))_
- As you can see from the way the dice are rolled, this is the process for a success roll with monster's attack points -7 as the difficulty . Therefore, a tie would result in character's evasion success = monster's attack failure . Also, if the game master rolls double ones or double sixes, that does not automatically result in failure/success (but for a character , it still does ). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11173))_
- Once the attack hits, it's the same. The monster's strike points are fixed, and the difference is based on protection from the character's armor. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11174))_
- Therefore, a tie would result in character's evasion success = monster's attack failure . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11173))_

### Chapter 16: / 16.1 Combat in Which Monsters Roll Dice / Attacks From Characters Against Monsters

- A score equal to a monster's evasion points - 7 is written next to it in parenthesis. This is used here. The game master should roll 2D and add it to the number in parenthesis. This is the target score for a character to hit the monster with an attack. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11176))_
- As in the previous section, this is the process for a success roll with monster's evasion points -7 as the difficulty . Therefore, a tie would result in character's attack success = monster's evasion failure . Please remember that the game master should be considered fi rst to roll the dice. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11177))_
- Once the attack hits, it's the same. A monster's defense points are fixed, and the difference is based on the character's strike roll. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11178))_
- Therefore, a tie would result in character's attack success = monster's evasion failure . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-0b9dec90-11177))_
