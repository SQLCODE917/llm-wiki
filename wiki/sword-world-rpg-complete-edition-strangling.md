Strangling is a combat technique used by various creatures in the Sword World RPG to constrict and kill opponents. It is a specific form of attack that involves using limbs or appendages to wrap around and suffocate a target.

In the context of the game, creatures such as octopuses and certain plants use strangling as their primary method of attack. The rules for strangling are detailed in the rulebook, and they typically involve specific mechanics for dealing damage and resolving the attack. For example, killer octopuses use all of their tentacles to attack one target, constricting the opponent with their entire body, and the rules for strangling are referenced in the monster entries for creatures like the killer octopus and giant octopus (p. 236).

The strangling technique is also used by other creatures such as grizzlies, which can use a "bear hug" that involves both strangling and biting the opponent at the same time (see [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition-chapter-13-2-7-animals-part-2]]). This attack is treated as strangling, and it is an important aspect of combat for these creatures.

Additionally, plants such as the creeping tree and killer creeper also utilize strangling as part of their attack mechanics. The creeping tree uses its tentacle-like branches to prey on animals, and tentacle attacks are resolved the same way as strangling. The killer creeper attacks by entangling with its vines, and if the attack hits, the vines will entangle the target, automatically dealing damage in subsequent rounds without requiring a hit check.

The mechanics of strangling are integral to understanding the combat abilities of these creatures and are an important concept for players and game masters to consider when designing encounters or determining the outcome of combat.
