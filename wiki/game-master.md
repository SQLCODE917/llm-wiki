# Game Master

The game master (GM) is the person who creates and facilitates the scenario in a role-playing game, making decisions and interpreting rules during gameplay. The GM is responsible for managing the fictional world and guiding the story, while the players control their individual characters.

## Role

- **Scenario Creation**: The GM prepares and presents the scenario or story that the players will navigate through.
- **World Management**: The GM maintains the consistency of the fictional world, including its rules, history, and inhabitants.
- **Rule Interpretation**: The GM interprets and applies the rules of the game, making final decisions when situations arise that aren't explicitly covered by the rulebook.
- **Narrative Facilitation**: The GM guides the story and helps players understand how their actions affect the world around them.

## Responsibilities

- **Preparation**: The GM must prepare scenarios, characters, and settings before the game begins.
- **Real-time Decision Making**: During gameplay, the GM must make quick decisions about how events unfold, especially when players take unexpected actions.
- **Balancing Gameplay**: The GM must ensure that the game remains balanced and enjoyable for all players.
- **Story Development**: The GM develops the narrative and responds to player choices in a way that maintains engagement and coherence.

## Relationship with Players

- **Director**: The GM acts as a director, orchestrating the game's events and ensuring that all players are engaged.
- **Judge**: The GM serves as the final arbiter of rules, applying them consistently to maintain fairness and clarity.
- **Facilitator**: The GM facilitates the overall experience, helping players understand the consequences of their actions and keeping the game moving forward.

## Key Concepts

- **Scenario**: A prepared situation or story that the GM uses to guide the game.
- **Character**: The role that a player assumes within the game world.
- **Success Roll**: A dice roll used to determine if a character's action succeeds or fails.
- **Dice**: Six-sided dice used for randomization in gameplay, with 1D and 2D rolls common.

## In Sword World RPG

In the context of Sword World RPG, the GM is responsible for creating scenarios and managing the game's flow. The GM must understand the rules in Part 2 of the rulebook, which are more detailed than those in Part 1, which are for players. The GM's role is crucial in making the game engaging and ensuring that players can make meaningful choices that affect the outcome of the story.
