In programming, **lazy evaluation** (or **non-strict evaluation**) is an evaluation strategy where expressions are evaluated only when their values are required, as opposed to **eager evaluation**, where expressions are evaluated immediately. This approach can optimize performance by avoiding unnecessary computations. The concept is discussed in *JavaScript Allongé* (raw/javascriptallonge.pdf p.289-304).
