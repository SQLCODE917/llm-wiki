## 18.3 Rules for Destroying Structures

Basically, characters cannot destroy walls or doors, but if you really want to implement something like this, it's fine for the game master to make decisions using his or her own common sense ("You have an ax, so you should be able to break down a wooden door"). However, if you specifically want to handle this numerically, please use the following rules.

## 18.3.1 How to Read the Structure Strength Table

### [Paper]

- **Defense points** = 0~3
- **Destruction points** = 1~10
- **Complete immunity** = Poison Gas-Type magic
- **No Criticals** = Bow, crossbow, cold-type magic
- **Normal** = Swords, spears, axes, maces, flails, slings, rocks, war hammers, barehanded, stone-type, wind cutter-type, blade-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Fire-type, electric-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic
- **Special** = If you cast "_Fire Weapon_" magic, strike power will become +20, and even weapons that cannot cause criticals will now be able to cause criticals. Also, if exposed to fire, it'll ignite immediately, and its defense points will automatically decrease by 1 point every round. When its defense points reach 0, its destruction points begin to decrease, and on the next round when destruction points reach 1, the paper object will burn out.

**Defense Points** indicate the strength of the material. There is a range, but the game master should decide on a specific score taking into consideration the thickness. If you cannot deal more damage than these minimum destruction points with a _single blow_, you will _not_ be able to destroy a structure with 0 defense points.

**Destruction Points** indicate the strength of the material, and the game master must determine a specific score depending on the thickness. The lowest of this score is called the _minimum destruction points_. In the case of paper, the score is 1~10, so the minimum destruction points are **1**.

### Example:

Consider trying to destroy a wooden wall (!) that's 20 cm thick. The game master considers this to have maximum strength for a wooden wall, and sets its _defense points_ and _destruction points_ to their maximums of _7_ and _15_ , respectively (see details below). Defense points and destruction points are used in the basic system for destroying structures.

If a character attacks with a weapon and deals _22 or greater_ points of damage in _one_ blow, this wooden wall will be destroyed. If the damage is _7 points or less_, the character's efforts are completely _wasted_. If the damage is _greater than 8_ but _less than 21 points_, the defense points will be reduced by _1 point_ (if the defense points become _0_, the destruction points will start to _decrease_. Once the destruction points reach _5 points_, they will _no longer_ decrease.)

When a character attempts to destroy something with a weapon, magic, etc., damage is calculated in the same way as when an attack hits a monster. Then, you subtract the defense points from the determined damage. If the result is _greater than or equal to_ the _destruction points_, the structure is _destroyed_ by that blow. If the result is _greater_ than 1 but _less_ than the destruction points, the _defense points_ are _reduced_ by 1 point. However, the structure itself is _not_ destroyed. If the result is _0 or less_ (when damage is less than or equal to defense points), that means there was _no_ effect at all. After the defense points fall to 0, the destruction points will decrease by 1 point. However, this will _only_ be reduced to the _minimum_ destruction points depending on the material. If you cannot deal more damage than these minimum destruction points with a _single blow_, you will _not_ be able to destroy a structure with 0 defense points.

**Complete Immunity, No Criticals, Normal, Ignore Defense Points** When it comes to destroying structures, some weapons and magic are completely unsuitable, while conversely, others are extremely effective. This is expressed by what's given here.

| Fire-type | Firebolt, Fireball, Firestorm |
|---|---|
| Cold-type | Blizzard, Ice Storm |
| Electric-type | Lightning, Lightning Bind |
| Stone-type | Stone Blast |
| Wind cutter-type | Windstorm |
| Earthquake-type | Crack, Earthquake |
| Explosion-type | Force, Force Explosion, Meteor Strike |
| Pure energy-type | Energy Bolt, Will-o-Wisp, Valkyrie Javelin |
| Poison gas-type | Acid Cloud |
| Blade-type | Blade Net |

If it says **complete immunity**, it can _never_ be destroyed by that weapon or magic.

If it says **no criticals**, bonus damage _cannot_ be dealt by a _critical_. Therefore, sometimes destruction is hopeless.

If it says **ignores defense points**, the defense points are _not_ taken into consideration, and if the damage _exceeds_ the destruction points, it'll be _destroyed in one blow_.

Otherwise, such attacks reduce defense points by 1 point (unless you roll _double ones_ on a strike roll).

Beyond this, game masters should also use common sense. There's no mention of entangling weapons, but there's no way they can do any damage.

_Crack_ is classified as earthquake-type, but given the way the magic is applied, it's unlikely that it'll be effective. Also, most walls don't move, so _Blade Net_ won't deal any damage.

**Special** Special notes are given in cases where special treatment is applied for that mate. Also, be aware of the number of people working as a group when creating holes. When making a hole large enough for a person to fit through, at most two people can work together.

## 18.3.2 Structure Material List

### [Wood]

- **Defense points** = 1-7
- **Destruction points** = 5-15
- **Complete Immunity** = Poison Gas-Type magic
- **No Criticals** = Swords, spears, bows, crossbows, barehanded, cold-type magic
- **Normal** = Axes, maces, flails, slings, rocks, war hammers, stone-type, wind cutter-type, blade-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Fire-type, electric-type, earthquake-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic
- **Special** = If you cast _Fire Weapon_ magic, not only will strike power increase by +10, even weapons that cannot cause criticals will now be able to cause criticals (defense points are effective). When you use fire magic or a weapon with _Fire Weapon_ cast on it, it will ignite if a critical occurs, and will be destroyed as a result.

### [Brick]

- **Defense points** = 3-30
- **Destruction points** = 10-20
- **Complete Immunity** = Spears, clubs, staves, bows, crossbows, fire-type, cold-type, electric-type, wind cutter-type, poison gas-type magic
- **No Criticals** = Swords, axes, slings, barehanded, blade-type magic
- **Normal** = Maces, flails, rocks, war hammers, stone-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Earthquake-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic

### [Stone]

- **Defense points** = 3-30
- **Destruction points** = 15-25
- **Complete Immunity** = Swords, spears, clubs, staves, bows, crossbows, slings, fire-type, cold-type, electric-type, wind cutter-type, poison gas-type magic
- **No Criticals** = Axes, maces, flails, rocks, barehanded, stone-type, blade-type magic
- **Normal** = War hammers
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Earthquake-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic

### [Iron]

- **Defense points** = 5-50
- **Destruction points** = 20-30
- **Complete Immunity** = Swords, axes, spears, clubs, staves, bows, crossbows, slings, rocks, barehanded, cold-type, electric-type, wind cutter-type, blade-type, poison gas-type magic
- **No Criticals** = Maces, flails, war hammers, stone-type, fire-type magic
- **Normal** = Pure energy-Type, earthquake-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Explosion-type magic
- **Special** = When destroyed with fire-type or pure energy-type magic, its temperature becomes extremely high immediately after destruction. If your character accidentally touches it, he or she will suffer severe burns. If you try this in a closed room, there's a great possibility your character will succumb to the heat before the wall is destroyed.

### [Ice]

- **Defense points** = 3-30
- **Destruction points** = 1-25
- **Complete Immunity** = Bows, crossbows, slings, cold-type, poison gas-type magic
- **No Criticals** = Swords, spears, clubs, staves, barehanded, electric-type, wind cutter-type, blade-type magic
- **Normal** = Axes, rocks, maces, flails, war hammers, stone-type, earthquake-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Fire-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic
- **Special** = If destroyed with fire-type or pure energy-type magic, it'll melt and turn into water.

### [Vegetation]

- **Defense points** = 3-30
- **Destruction points** = 10-20
- **Complete Immunity** = Bows, crossbows, slings, barehanded, stone-type, earthquake-type magic
- **No Criticals** = Swords, spears, clubs, staves, cold-type, poison gas-type magic
- **Normal** = Axes, rocks, maces, flails, war hammer, electric-type, wind cutter-type, blade-type magic
- **Ignore Defense Points** = Fire-type, explosion-type, pure energy-type magic
- **Special** = If attacked with fire-type magic or a weapon with _Fire Weapon_ cast on it, treat it the same as wood.

## 18.3.3 Handling Spirit Walls

When using these rules for destroying structures to destroy the spirit magic Spirit Wall, please do so as follows.

### [Spirit Wall: Gnome]

Treat it as a _stone_ wall. Without expansion, defense points are 15 and destruction points are 20. Its minimum destruction points are 1 because it's stone.

### [Spirit Wall: Frau]

Treat it as an _ice_ wall. Without expansion, defense points are 15 and destruction points are 20. Its minimum destruction points are 1 because it's ice.

### [Spirit Wall: Dryad]

Treat it as a _vegetation_ wall. Without expansion, defense points and destruction points are both 15.

_Spirit Wall: Dryad_ has the power to regenerate even if it's destroyed. Each round, _Spirit Wall: Dryad_ regenerates 1 defense point and 1 destruction point.
