## Chapter 2.7: Resistance Rolls

A **resistance roll** is a special type of check that uses adventurer level in its baseline score calculation.

### Life Force Resistance Roll

A success roll made using _adventurer level + life force bonus_ as the baseline score is specifically referred to as a **life force resistance roll**. These rolls are made to avoid or reduce the effects of physical dangers such as poison.

### Mental Power Resistance Roll

A success roll made using _adventurer level + mental power bonus_ as the baseline score is specifically called a **mental power resistance roll**. These rolls are made to avoid or reduce the effects of magic or curses. All checks against magic, even if it causes physical damage, are made with mental power resistance rolls. Checks against special attacks by monsters that are magical in nature are also made with mental power resistance rolls.

### Baseline Score Calculation

The baseline score (resistance) equals _adventurer level + life force/mental power bonus_.

When a character with adventurer level 0 (a character with no adventurer skills) makes these checks, the baseline score is _0_ regardless of their ability scores.

### Example

**Example 1:** Ducard II has an adventurer level of 2 and a +3 life force bonus. The baseline score of his life force resistance roll is _5_.

**Example 2:** Ducard II has an adventurer level of 2 and a +2 mental power bonus. The baseline score of his mental power resistance roll is _4_.

### Character Sheet

The two resistance rolls are frequently performed. Therefore, it’s advisable to make a note of the baseline score (resistance) so that it is readily available. The Character Sheet provides a field for this purpose. Look at the life force and mental power fields under ability scores. To the right of each of these is a field to write down the bonus, and further to the right of that should be a field labeled _resistance_ . Write the baseline scores of each resistance roll here.
