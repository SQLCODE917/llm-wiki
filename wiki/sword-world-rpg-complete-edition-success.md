## Statements

### Chapter 2: Success Rolls

- The baseline score is a number that represents a character's ability. However, the success of an action is not only related to the ability of the person attempting it, but also to the difficulty of the action itself. The difficulty of hitting a target with the same weapon is much greater when your opponent is a seasoned warrior, than when he is an amateur with no fighting experience. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00553))_

### Chapter 2: Success Rolls / 2.5 Difficulty Checks

- On a success roll, if a player rolls a **12** , i.e., both dice roll 6 ( **double sixes** ), the action is a _success_ , no matter how low the baseline score nor how high the target score. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00577))_

- Basically, automatic success and automatic failure are _not_ definitive success or definitive failure. It simply means that you succeeded or failed, _not_ that you succeeded very _well_ or failed very _badly_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00578))_

### Chapter 2: Success Rolls

- A success roll may be subject to modifiers. This is to replicate any favorable or unfavorable circumstances when a character is about to perform an action. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00582))_

- In such cases, the game master applies a **bonus** or **penalty** to the success roll. Things like, _+2 because they're falling over_ , or _-4 because it's dark_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00583))_

- Some games treat extreme stakes during an action check as a _definitive_ success or failure, and _Sword World RPG_ may do so as well. However, if such a process is _not_ specified, it is _not_ treated as a definitive success/failure. Do be careful. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00600))_

### Chapter 2: Success Rolls / Analogy of Success and Failure

- If a success roll is made for a difficulty check, the success or failure of the action is strictly unknown to the player. That is precisely the reason why we use difficulty checks. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00623))_
