## Skills

In addition to ability scores, _Sword World RPG_ also uses numeric values called **skills** to describe the characteristics of characters. Skills have a greater impact on a character's actions than ability scores. This chapter explains the basics of skills.

### 1.3.1 Skill Basics

There are several types of skills, which are divided into several groups. For each skill, a character's proficiency is represented by a **level** from 0~10. 0 levels indicates that someone is completely unrelated to that skill, while 10 levels represents the highest proficiency that a normal human can attain.

Skills are referred to by names such as _fighter_ , _thief_ , _merchant_ , etc. As you can see, they have a strong connotation of professional job functions rather than skills. It is also possible for one character to acquire (have levels in) more than one skill.

Those who have these skills are often referred to by their skill names. So instead of _someone who has the fighter skill_ , they are simply called _fighters_ .

Half-elves have a longer life span than humans due to their elven characteristics, with a life span of around 200 years.

Half-elves vary in their traits depending on whether they were raised by humans or by elves. When raised by elves, they're similar to those of elves. When raised by humans, they're not all that different from humans.

### 1.3.2 Skill Types

Skills are grouped into several types. Below is an explanation.

First, skills are divided into two types: **adventurer skills** and **general skills** . Adventurer skills, as the name implies, are acquired by player characters, the adventurers, or by equivalent characters. There are eight of these: **fighter** , **thief** , **priest** , **sorcerer** , **shaman** , **sage** , **ranger** , and **bard** . There are also two adventurer skills that _cannot_ be acquired by a player character: **dark priest** and **dragon priest** .

General skills are skills of an ordinary person as opposed to an adventurer, such as **merchant** , **farmer** , etc. Some of these are useful during an adventure, and it is well worth it for an adventurer to acquire general skills.

Each skill consists of a number of abilities appropriate to the skill name, and having a **skill level** (1 skill level or higher) means that you can demonstrate those abilities.

### 1.3.3 Adventurer Level

In Sword World RPG, a character's capability as an adventurer is determined by their adventurer skill level. The highest level among the adventurer skills that a character has is called that character's **adventurer level** . For example, a character with priest skill level 1, fighter skill level 4 (these are adventurer skills), sailor skill level 5, and fisher skill level 2 (these are general skills) would be at adventurer level **4** .

### 1.3.4 Explanation of Adventurer Skills

Adventurer skills are the most important thing for an adventurer in _Sword World RPG._ This is because which adventurer skills a character has acquired will determine what that character can do, and will reveal that character's characteristics.

Below is a brief description of each adventurer skill.

## Fighter

Those with this skill are adept warriors, who fight with weapons. They are experts in combat, able to use all kinds of weapons and armor.

_A wandering swordsman who makes a name for himself solely on the strength of his sword, and eventually establishes a kingdom._ Such a story is also possible in Forcelia.

## Thief

One who has this skill will be called a _thief_ . As indicated, they bring along abilities that are useful for theft, such as pickpocketing, lock-picking, and so on.

However, not everyone with the thief skill makes a living off such thefts. The abilities of the thief are also useful in adventures. For example, there's the ability to detect and disable traps that have been set, and the ability to stay ahead of footsteps, listening and observing what’s going on.

Thieves also have some fighting ability. Their fighting style is based on lightness, and although they cannot use heavy weapons or armor, they _can_ use light weapons and armor that does not impede their movement, allowing them to fight as well as a fighter.

For this reason, many of them have made a name for themselves as _adventurers_ .

## Sage

Those with the sage skill are commonly referred to as sages. They're knowledgeable and well-versed in all fields of study (academics in Forcelia are not differentiated, and one is rarely versed in only one specific field).

## Bard

Those who play musical instruments and sing songs for a living are called bards. Thus, those with the bard skill have the ability to play an instrument and sing songs. Many legends and folklore are often woven into songs passed down from ancient times, and natural bards are familiar with these legends and folklore.

Bards also have the ability to sing magical songs called **spellsongs** . These songs have magical effects on all who hear them.

## Ranger

An expert in outdoor activities. Those with this skill are adept at outdoor living, and dealing with a variety of natural hazards. These abilities are, of course, essential for _adventure_ .

Rangers are also excellent with projectile weapons and have the same offensive capabilities as fighters, especially when it comes to projectiles.

## Rune Master

The five adventurer skills of sorcerer, shaman, priest, dark priest, and dragon priest are sometimes collectively referred to as **rune master skills** . This is because these five classes use **runes** and have the ability to handle magic. Rune masters are viewed with fear by the general public because of their magical abilities.

Bards also use magical abilities through their _spellsongs_ but are not considered rune masters. There is a great difference in the _spells_ that rune masters handle, both in quality and quantity.

## Sorcerer

The ancient kingdom of Kastuul, which fell more than 500 years ago, is sometimes referred to as the _magical kingdom_ . As its nickname suggests, Kastuul is said to have accomplished various miracles through the power of magic. It is this magical power that is known as **high ancient** and is the magic that the sorcerers of today have inherited.

With the fall of Kastuul, much of high ancient was lost, but some has survived to the present day. The skill that can handle it is the sorcerer skill.

Many sorcerers are also sages. The rules do not require one character to acquire both of these skills, but if they are acquired at the same time, it gives them a significant advantage.

## Shaman

It is said that when creating Forcelia, the gods divided the world into three: the _spirit world_ , the _fairy world_ , and the _material world_ . The _spirit world_ is the world of **spirits** who command various powers. The _material world_ is the world in which physical beings like us humans live, and the _fairy world_ is the world that connects the two.

**Silent spirit** are runes used to communicate with spirits and draw out the powers they control. The skill that can use silent spirit is called the shaman skill, and those who have this skill are referred to as shamans.

The powers controlled by spirits are diverse, ranging from the four major powers of _earth_ , _water_ , _fire_ , and _wind_ to the _spirit of humans_ , and there are many different types of **spirit magic** used by shamans.

## Priest

Those who exercise their power as agents of the gods are priests. In the ancient days of myth, the gods who created the world were later divided into light and darkness and waged a great war. A great war that engulfed all the ancient races– including the legendary dragons of old --ended with all the gods losing their bodies.

Gods who have lost their physical bodies cannot engage with the _material world_ , which is the world for those with bodies. Instead, they interact with the world through priests.

Priests, as agents of the gods, can use **holy prayer** and cast **holy magic** .

## Dark Priest

The gods are divided into two main groups. The **gods of light** and the **gods of darkness** . This is the same classification as the two factions that waged war in ancient times.

Gods who belonged to the _gods of darkness_ are now considered _wicked gods_ . Each of the gods teaches and guides people, but the teachings and guidance of the _gods of darkness_ are extremely individualistic and deny human reason, because they are far removed from human _morality_ , so to speak. Another hindrance to belief in the wicked gods, is the fact that the majority of people in Forcelia today believe in the teachings of the _gods of light_ .

However, even the wicked gods have their believers. As agents of the wicked gods, there are dark priests who use **daemon scream** and cast **dark magic** , which is essentially equivalent to Divine Magic. Players may _not_ choose the dark priest skill as one of their character's skills. This is because it would be significantly more difficult to have a character practice the teachings of the _gods of darkness_ , when examining a roleplaying game from the _role-playing_ perspective.

## Dragon Priest

The runes used by dragon priests are called **dragon roar** .

Dragon roar is a system of magic that imbues one's own body with the characteristics of a dragon, and the ultimate goal is to transform oneself into a dragon. Therefore, they worship dragons and try to live like them.

Players may _not_ choose the dragon priest skill as one of their character's skills. This is because it would be extremely difficult to have a character live the life of a dragon priest, when examining a roleplaying game from the _role-playing_ perspective.
