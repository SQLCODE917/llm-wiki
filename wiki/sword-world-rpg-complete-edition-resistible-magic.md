In Sword World RPG, there are two kinds of magic: **resistible** and **non-resistible**.

## Non-resistible Magic

Non-resistible magic can be effective regardless of what the target does. To use non-resistible magic, an adventurer simply declares which magic they're using and rolls 2D. If the result is anything other than double ones, the magic takes effect. If it is double ones, the magic takes no effect due to some error in casting.

## Resistible Magic

Resistible magic can be rendered ineffective or less effective if the target chooses to resist. When casting resistible magic on a monster, the caster must make a success roll using their magic power as the baseline score and the monster's mental point resistance as the target score.

If 2D roll + magic power ≥ monster's mental point resistance, the magic takes full effect.
If 2D roll + magic power < monster's mental point resistance, the magic takes an incomplete effect or fails completely.

If the 2D roll comes up double ones, the magic fails completely regardless of the magic power.

If the 2D roll comes up double sixes, the magic automatically succeeds and takes full effect, no matter how high the monster's mental point resistance.

## Casting on Other Adventurers

When an adventurer casts resistible magic on another adventurer, the same principles apply but with mental power resistance instead of mental point resistance. The caster rolls 2D and adds their magic power to determine the attack strength, while the target rolls 2D and adds their mental power resistance to determine the defense strength. The comparison determines the outcome.

## Casting on Mixed Groups

When casting magic on a group that includes both adventurers and monsters, the rules are more complex and require special handling as outlined in the optional rules under 17.1: Changes to Magic Procedure.
