# AoE2 Scouting Techniques

Scouting in Age of Empires II is the process of using the scout and later map vision to identify resources, enemy activity, base layout, tech choices, and upgrades.

## Source-backed details

| Claim | Evidence | Locator | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manual scouting is preferred over auto-scout for efficient scouting results. | "you might be tempted to just press the auto scout button and be done with it, but this is not really a way to get efficient results with scouting." | `normalized:L260` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| The scout should look for player resources, enemy activity, and extra resources on the map. | "what you actually should be doing with the scout is actually looking for your resources, what enemy is doing and where are the extra resources in the map." | `normalized:L260` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| At the start of the game, the scout should first reveal the landscape around the player's town center. | "So when the game starts your goal is to scout the landscape around your TC first" | `normalized:L262` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Enemy scouting asks where the enemy's golds, woodlines, berries, hills, and similar map features are. | "where is his golds, woodlines, berries, hills and so on forth." | `normalized:L264` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Later scouting should identify enemy buildings, unit tech choices, and unit upgrades. | "Later in the game you want to use scouting to know what your opponent is doing, which buildings does he have or what units he is teching into and what sort of upgrades his units have." | `normalized:L268` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| The scouting pattern is to move the screen to the unit, click forward to a visible spot, go back to economy tasks, and repeat. | "when you move your scout, you move your screen to where the scout is on the map (preferably using control group) click the scout forward to a spot that the scout actually has vision on and go back to your eco and do things and repeat." | `normalized:L270` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |

## Steps

1. Avoid relying on auto-scout when efficient scouting is needed.
2. Scout around the town center first to reveal the immediate landscape and starting resources.
3. Use the scout to find player resources, enemy activity, and extra resources on the map.
4. When scouting the enemy, look for golds, woodlines, berries, hills, and similar base features.
5. Later in the game, check enemy buildings, unit tech choices, and upgrades.
6. Repeat the screen-to-scout loop: move the screen to the scout, click it toward a visible forward spot, return to economy tasks, and repeat.
