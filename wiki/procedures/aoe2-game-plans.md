# AoE2 Game Plans

A game plan in Age of Empires II is a strategic plan built from map generation, civilization power spikes, resource advantages, and the opponent's likely responses.

## Source-backed details

| Claim | Evidence | Locator | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Game plans start from map generation and civilization power spikes. | "We take into consideration map generation and civ power spikes and start moving from there, that is how we make game plans." | `normalized:L541` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| A game plan can combine an opening, a unit mix, and a timing goal. | "So game plans are a combination of everything, you open man-at-arms with the intent to play heavy feudal with two ranges and archer skirmisher mix, in order to get a lead in the match up which could be devastating in castle age." | `normalized:L543` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Opponent civilization strengths can create a timer that the game plan must answer. | "The civilization is very strong economically and will often get ridiculous resource leads over the course of the game and by the time imperial age comes into the picture, they can already wipe you off of the map at ease. So you're essentially put on a timer to not allow the enemy to just boom away right?" | `normalized:L543` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| A civilization-specific resource advantage can shape the planned unit sequence. | "So you'll build your gameplan on the fact that you are saving resources in buildings and gaining eco lead and play man-at-arms into archers into xbow + knights in castle age in an attempt to leverage your resource advantage to full capacity and get opponent out of the game." | `normalized:L545` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| Vulnerable enemy woodlines are a map-generation feature that can shape the plan. | "Now how does map generation work with game planning? You recognize that the enemy has vulnerable woodlines as an example, he has two close woodlines in front of his base." | `normalized:L547` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |
| If the enemy has all golds forward, the plan may need to track extra golds and solidify control over front golds. | "In some cases, like an enemy having all golds forward, you have keep track of the extra golds out on the map and figure out a way to solidify your control over those front golds while knowing that the opponent WILL try to reclaim them either during feudal age or early castle age most likely." | `normalized:L549` | [Noobs guide for AoE2](../sources/aoe2-basics.md) |

## Steps

1. Check map generation for exposed resources, especially woodlines and forward golds.
2. Identify both civilizations' power spikes and economic advantages.
3. Choose an opening and unit mix that fits the matchup and intended timing.
4. Use civilization-specific resource advantages to shape the unit sequence.
5. Plan how to control or deny key resources the opponent will need to reclaim.
