# Power

What [[sword-world-rpg-complete-edition]] covers about power:

## Statements

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Mental Power 】

- Mental power represents the adventurer's willpower and perseverance. Using magic is hard work, and one cannot hope to cast much magic without a high mental capacity. Mental power is also related to magic resistance. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00164))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / Loss of Ability Scores and Its Impact

- While adventuring, adventurers often suffer physical and mental injuries and fatigue. In _Sword World RPG_ , this is represented by a loss of life force and mental power (point reduction). If either life force or mental power fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will fall unconscious and be unable to act. The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. At this time, whether they're conscious or not depends on the situation. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00168))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.1 Ability Scores / 【 Intelligence 】

- If life force and mental power are reduced by wounds or exhaustion, they will recover once the wounds are treated or the exhaustion is wiped away. There are various conditions for these reductions and recoveries, which you will discover as you read through this book. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00173))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / 【 Shaman 】

- The powers controlled by spirits are diverse, ranging from the four major powers of _earth_ , _water_ , _fire_ , and _wind_ to the _spirit of humans_ , and there are many different types of **spirit magic** used by shamans. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00276))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.3 Skills / 【 Priest 】

- Those who exercise their power as agents of the gods are priests. In the ancient days of myth, the gods who created the world were later divided into light and darkness and waged a great war. A great war that engulfed all the ancient races– including the legendary dragons of old --ended with all the gods losing their bodies. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00278))_

### Chapter 1: Character / 1.4 Character Creation / 【 Half-Elf 】

- Half-elves are halfway between human and elf in terms of ability. Therefore, their dexterity, agility, intelligence, and mental power are slightly higher, while their strength and life force are slightly lower. However, they do not deviate very far from the average and are fully capable of handling all skills. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00315))_

### Chapter 4: Weapon Combat / 4.4 Scores Used in Weapon Combat / 【 Strike Power 】

- Strike power is the same number as the _required strength_ of the weapon used. However, if the weapon is a _two-handed_ weapon, it's instead _required strength + 5_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00846))_

### Chapter 4: Weapon Combat / 4.4 Scores Used in Weapon Combat / 【 Defense Power 】

- Defense power is the same number as the _required strength_ of the armor worn by the character. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-00859))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / When an Adventurer Casts Magic on Adventurers

- First, the side casting the magic rolls 2D. The final score (magic power + 2D) must then be determined this one time only. ### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / 5.1.6 Recovering Mental Power / Example:

- The evil sorcerer Gorman (magic power 5) unleashes _Lightning_ on the three adventurers. First, Gorman rolls 2D and adds his own magic power. If we assume his 2D roll is _8_ , each of the three adventurers must make a mental power resistance roll, with a target score of 5 (magic power) + 8 (roll) = _13_ . Suppose that the baseline score of all three of their mental power resistance rolls is _4_ . If their rolls are each _2_ (double ones!), _6_ , and _9_ , then only the _last_ one is able to resist the _Lightning_ . _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01262))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Consuming Mental Power

- Each magic has a score called **base mental power cost** . This base mental power cost, divided by the corresponding rune master skill level (rounded up), equals the actual mental power consumed by the character. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01273))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 5.1 Basic Rules of Magic / Example:

- A character with shaman skill level 2 uses the _Fire Bolt_ spell. The base mental power cost for _Fire Bolt_ is _5_ . The character must consume 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5, rounding up to _3_ points of mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01275))_

- A character can reduce their mental power to 0, but they _cannot_ make it negative. For example, a character with 5 points of mental power remaining _can_ use magic that costs 5 points of mental power, but _cannot_ use magic that costs _6 or more_ points of mental power. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01276))_

- If a character’s mental power reaches _0_ , they will _lose consciousness_ . The character cannot regain consciousness unless they recover their mental power to _1 point or more_ in some way. _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01277))_

### Chapter 5: Magic / 《 Magic Power Decrease 》

- Magic power can be optionally reduced. Also, if the side casting the magic rolls double sixes, the resisting side will _also_ need double sixes, no matter _how much_ magic power was declared to be decreased (if it's a monster, it _cannot_ resist). _(Sword World RPG - Complete Edition.pdf (source-range-870a16bc-01349))_
