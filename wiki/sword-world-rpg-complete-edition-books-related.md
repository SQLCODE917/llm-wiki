# Books Related to Sword World RPG

Here, we'll introduce some books that will help you understand _Sword World RPG_ . You do not need all of these, but knowing their contents can make playing _Sword World RPG_ more enjoyable. 

## 1. Replay Collections

The _Sword World RPG Replay Collections_ , published by Fujimi Dragon Books, are a good way to learn how to actually play the game. The progress of the game is reproduced via conversation between the game master and the players. 

Once you read these, you will quickly understand how the game is meant to progress. 

There are three points to be aware of, however. 

First, _Sword World RPG Replay Collections_ ① _~_ ⑨ are _not_ based on this rulebook, but rather on the previously released _old_ version of the rules (see below). Since some of the rules in this book you’re reading right now have been revised, there are rare cases where the descriptions are contradictory. When you play, please follow what's written in _these_ rules. 

Second, although the _Duda RPG Replay Collection_ series uses the rules of _Sword World RPG_ , it is a variant that emphasizes the aspects of humor, fantasy and mystery. As a game, it’s nearly the same, but in this series, modified rules not found in _Sword World RPG_ , such as **inept detective checks** , have been introduced to demonstrate its uniqueness. So please note that even though it's 99% Sword World RPG, it's still not 100% (for more information on this, please read the Introduction and Commentary sections of _Duda RPG Replay Collection_ ① _: The Lost Detective Appears!_ ). 

Finally, these replays are based on actual play and reproduction of the situations, but have been cut or edited for redundancy and other aspects in order to prioritize readability. Some rule errors that occurred during the game have been corrected and dealt with, especially in areas where they do not have a major impact. In actual play, things do not go as smoothly as this. A replay should _not_ be considered a complete manual, but merely a reference. 

## 2. Scenario Collections

The _Sword World RPG Scenario Collections_ , published by Fujimi Dragon Books, save game masters the trouble of creating their own scenarios. Not only will they save you from creating scenarios, but they will also serve as a useful model when you do create your own. 

Please note that some of these follow the _old_ version of rules. However, since there are almost no revisions, they can be used nearly as-is. 

Although the details of monsters and magical items listed in individual scenarios may differ from the rules in this book, when using them, please play with the details as described in the scenario, with the exception of item prices. This is because the information in this book has been modified for more general use. For this reason, there may be times when game balance within the scenario is not fully supported. Please follow this rule for item prices only. 

## 3. World Guide

The _Sword World RPG World Guide_ , published by Fujimi Shobo, is a comprehensive introduction to the continent of Alecrast, the background setting of the game. It is very useful when creating your own scenarios, and for those who are mainly players, it is also effective to have a general outline of the world. 

## 4. Sword World Novels and Short Story Collections

_Sword World Novels_ and _Sword World Short Story Collections_ published by Fujimi Fantasia Bunko are a suitable introduction to the world of _Sword World RPG_ . 

It’ll be helpful to know what the setting of _Sword World RPG_ is like and what kind of thoughts the people who live there are thinking. 

However, among these, the _Duda Series_ and _Dawn of Chaos_ `Ⅰ` _-_ `Ⅳ` are slightly different. The former, as mentioned in the Replay Collection section, is a humorous mystery variant, so the books emphasize this aspect. The latter is set not on the continent of Alecrast, the main setting of _Sword World RPG_ , but rather in a land called **Chaos Land** . All of these require some ingenuity to handle with this rulebook alone. However, the underlying setting of Forcelia is still common. 

## 5. Sword World RPG Adventures

The _Sword World RPG Adventures_ series, published by Fujimi Dragon Books, are collections of ideas from readers who play _Sword World RPG_ , in the form of novels. Similar to the _Sword World Novels/Short Story Collections_ , these are a good way to learn about the setting. 

## 6. Collection Series

The _Collection Series_ , such as _Monster Collection_ published by Fujimi Dragon Books, are a good way to learn the terminology used within the game in _Sword World RPG_ . 

This game uses terms such as _goblin_ and _broadsword_ , which appear frequently in fantasy games, but are otherwise not so common. Explaining each of these terms one by one would not only make the rulebook thicker, but would also hinder understanding of the rules themselves. 

Therefore, this rulebook keeps explanations of such terms to a minimum. To learn more about these terms, please refer to the _Collection Series_ . 

## 7. Old Version of the Rules

Three volumes have been published by Fujimi Dragon Books: _Sword World RPG_ , and _Sword World RPG Advanced Rules Part 1_ and _2_ . These are the rulebooks for _Sword World RPG_ , that is, they serve exactly the same purpose as this book. This book is a re-edited and partially revised version of the contents of these three books, so they're unnecessary if you’re holding this in your hands. 

## 8. Game Master Screen

The _Sword World RPG Game Master Screen_ is a collection of charts (or tables) that you will need in order to play this game. This can be useful for smooth game progress. 

However, it is in accordance with the _old_ version of the rules mentioned above. In this book, some rules have been revised and some tables have been slightly changed. Therefore, it's advisable to make some modifications when using this. For example, you can copy the revised tables and paste them over the old ones. 

In addition to these, _Sword World RPG_ is scheduled to feature a variety of other related works. It will also continue to be supported in magazines published by Fujimi Shobo, such as _Dragon Magazine_ . 

## Part 1: Players’ Section

### Chapter 1: Character

#### Ability Scores

Just as there are many different kinds of people in the real world, strong (or weak), smart (or dumb), etc., there are many different kinds of people in Forcelia. In Sword World RPG, these basic characteristics of an adventurer are represented by numbers called **ability scores** . There are six types of ability scores: **dexterity** , **agility** , **intelligence** , **strength** , **life force** , and **mental power** . The higher the number for each, the better the ability. Ability scores apply not only to humans, but also to the various non-human races that become adventurers. Human ability scores range from 4-24 (14 on average) for each. 

##### Life Force

Life force represents how well an adventurer can withstand physical injury. The higher this number, the more robust the adventurer is. 

##### Mental Power

Mental power represents the adventurer's willpower and perseverance. Using magic is hard work, and one cannot hope to cast much magic without a high mental capacity. Mental power is also related to magic resistance. 

##### Dexterity

Dexterity represents the dexterity of an adventurer's hands. It relates to a variety of tasks that involve the use of one's hands. This is especially important when wielding weapons in combat. 

While adventuring, adventurers often suffer physical and mental injuries and fatigue. In _Sword World RPG_ , this is represented by a loss of life force and mental power (point reduction). If either life force or mental power fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will fall unconscious and be unable to act. The four other ability scores of dexterity, agility, intelligence, and strength are rarely reduced, but if this does happen and these ability scores _do_ fall to 0 or less, the adventurer will still become disabled. At this time, whether they're conscious or not depends on the situation. 

##### Agility

Agility represents an adventurer's physical balance and agility. It's related to working with the whole body. In combat, it is important when evading enemy attacks. 

##### Intelligence

Intelligence represents the speed of an adventurer's mind and the amount of knowledge they hold, or in other words, their so-called smarts. It also represents the ability to perceive things, such as finding hidden objects and sensing abnormalities spur of the moment. Above all, it is important when using magic, a major attraction in fantasy. 

If life force and mental power are reduced by wounds or exhaustion, they will recover once the wounds are treated or the exhaustion is wiped away. There are various conditions for these reductions and recoveries, which you will discover as you read through this book. 

##### Strength

Strength represents an adventurer's muscular strength. If you want to use large weapons, heavy armor, etc., high strength is required. 

#### Loss of Ability Scores and Its Impact

An ability score value is related to the success or failure of an action that an adventurer tries to perform, but in _Sword World RPG_ , success or failure is determined by a **bonus** derived from the ability score, rather than the ability score itself. The result of each ability score divided by 6 (rounded down) will be the bonus for that ability score (Table 1-1: Ability Bonuses). Each of these numbers is referred to as a _dexterity bonus_ , _intelligence bonus_ , etc., using the name of the ability score. For example, it would say something like _an adventurer with 14 dexterity has a dexterity bonus of +2_ (of course, the bonus is always 0 or a positive number, but by nature it's usually written ±0, +1, +2...... etc.) When a character's ability score increases or decreases, even temporarily, the ability bonus increases or decreases accordingly. However, temporary reductions do _not_ affect modifiers for only two scores: life force and mental power.

#### Races

Humans are not the only adventurers in Forcelia. Next, we'll explain the races that are relatively common as adventurers in Forcelia, though they’re not as common as humans. 

Players are free to choose from humans or any of these races to be their alter ego, an adventurer. 

##### Dwarf

Dwarves were once members of the fae tribe who lived in the fairy world, but now they have fully immersed into the world inhabited by humans (which we call the material world) and no longer have the ability to travel to and from the fairy world. Their lives are no different from those of humans. 

They are about 120 cm tall, round and plump, and have bulbous noses. Men often have long beards on their chins, while women do not. Their hair ranges from brown to dark brown and their eyes are a similar color. Their skin color is dark, almost brown. 

They can see things in the dark as well as they can in daylight. They are also very dexterous with their hands, and excel in such techniques as craftsmanship and blacksmithing. Many of their works are stunning and cannot be imitated by humans. Dwarves usually live in human cities and villages using their own skills, but there are several cities and villages where only dwarves live. 

They have a very strong sense of tribalism, respecting the rules of their tribe and seldom violating them. They mainly worship the gods of the earth, with **Braki** as their main deity, who is also the god of smithing. 

The life span of a dwarf is longer than that of a human, thought to be around 200 years. 

Their rule characteristics are as follows: 
2. Among the craftsman general skill, has any one of the following at level 5: carpentry, smithing, woodworking, stonemasonry, gem crafting (details are explained under 7.3: Craftsman Skill, see p. 126). 
3. Unable to learn ancient magic or spirit magic. 

##### Grassrunner

Grassrunners were originally members of the fae tribe who lived in the meadows. However, they are very curious and many have abandoned the fairy world to become residents of human society. They have already lost the ability to return to the fairy world, but as fae of the meadows, they have a truly diverse range of abilities. 

They are about 1 meter tall and have the appearance of a human child. Other than their slightly pointed ears, they are not much different from humans. Their hair varies from golden to tan. Their eyes have a similar color. Their skin color is predominantly black or reddish. 

They’re not in the habit of settling in one place. They only stay in one place for a while only to raise a few children, and once the children are grown, they travel again. They usually lead a nomadic lifestyle, either alone or in family units. Of course, they have no concept of tribes, nor do they like to live in cities. Their personalities are very cheerful and simple. Therefore, they have no custom of believing in gods at all. What they desire are the various experiences and incidents one encounters while traveling, so for this reason they may join groups of adventurers. 

Grassrunners have a life span of about 200 years. 

Their rule characteristics are as follows: 
1. Has 1 level in the thief skill. 
3. Can communicate with plants and insects. 
4. Cannot learn any runes. 

##### Elf

Elves are members of the fae tribe who live in the forests, and are the envy of humans because of their beautiful appearance and immortality (no life span). 

Their most distinctive feature is their long, slender, pointy ears. They're about 160 cm tall and slim. Their body hair is thin, and even men do not grow beards. Their hair color ranges from golden to chestnut, sometimes silver. Their skin color is near white and their eyes are pale blue or green. They are also close as a species to humans and both can produce offspring. These children are called **halfelves** . 

They live a very closed-off life in the forest. They do not like outsiders, nor do they want to be ruled by others. As such, they may act aggressively toward intruders into the forest. They rarely leave the forests where they live, but occasionally the odd elf will show up in town. They do not believe in any gods. 

One of the reasons humans envy elves is their _immortality_ , which is a characteristic of an elite (ancient) race called **high elves** . However, these high elves avoid contact with humans even more so than common elves. Elves who appear in the cities of humans are for the most part ordinary (newer species) elves, bound to a life span of about 1000 years rather than immortality. 

The rule characteristics of elves are as follows: 
1. Has 1 level in the shaman skill 
2. Unable to learn holy magic. 
3. Has 1 level in the ranger skill. 
4. Can see in the dark.
