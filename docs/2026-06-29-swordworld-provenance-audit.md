## Interpretation

- The clean two-layer fix is in effect: document-structure repair now keeps split numbered headings attached to their real titles, and Projection Context now coalesces adjacent mid-sentence evidence blocks while preserving multi-range citations.
- The `sword-world-rpg-complete-edition-acquiring-rune-master-skill` page now has an authoritative 11.4 page shape, and the Dark Priest Skill paragraph renders as one coherent statement citing both `source-range-0b9dec90-06318` and `source-range-0b9dec90-06320`.
- The `sword-world-rpg-complete-edition-knight` page is a good control case: it has no audit findings, two relevant statements, and one justified related page to `noble`.
- Excluding the source hub page, the audit covers 1,246 generated Sword World pages, 20,677 cited source-range items, and 15,001 findings.
- The dominant non-source signal is `structure-only-range` with 13,865 findings. These are traceable source-coverage segments, not missing citations, but they show that many section/source pages still project source-support text that did not become normalized Claim Ledger entries or technical atoms.
- `fragmentary-statement` dropped to 67 non-source findings after coalescing. Remaining cases are ledger-level fragments; the display layer can hide some, but the better next root fix is to move continuity repair earlier so the normalized ledger entries are not fragmentary.
- `range-order-outlier` is mostly broad concept aggregation, especially pages like ability score, adventurer level, target score, and bonus damage. Some of that is useful, but broad concept pages should probably become link-first summaries with grouped subtopic bands rather than long mixed-order evidence dumps.
- `topic-support-gap` and `technical-atom-topic-gap` are now small on non-source pages. The remaining cases point to over-broad lexical topic matching and table/atom attachment that should be constrained by section identity and role labels.
- The source hub page is a different kind of page: it is effectively a source manifest/dump, and it accounts for most global findings. It should be audited separately from concept and section pages.
- The portable `claim-ledger.json` omits `source_statements`, while rendered pages rely on Projection Context evidence blocks built in memory. For portable auditability, source statements or Projection Context should become a first-class serialized artifact.

## Higher-Level Decisions

- Make page projection source-of-truth explicit by page family. Concept pages should be claim-ledger/topic-index projections. Section pages should either be labeled and treated as source-coverage reference pages, or they should be projected only through claim-ledger evidence blocks and technical atom frames.
- Promote Projection Context from renderer helper to a durable system artifact. It is the right home for coherent evidence passages, multi-range citations, and technical-atom context.
- Add a projection gate for standalone unresolved pointers: deictic entries are acceptable inside a coherent evidence block, but should not be rendered as isolated claims.
- Add a source-coverage-to-ledger coverage budget: pages with many structure-only ranges should be flagged as source-summary pages, not concept pages.
- Keep broad pages dense but walkable by grouping distant evidence by section path and using related pages as first-class navigation, instead of letting every matching source range appear as a flat claim list.
